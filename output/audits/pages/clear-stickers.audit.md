<h2>Clear Sticker Frequently Asked Questions</h2>

<h3>What makes clear stickers different from regular stickers?</h3>
<p>Clear stickers use transparent vinyl material that allows background visibility while displaying your printed design. This makes them ideal for window applications where maintaining see-through properties is important. The transparent areas of your design remain completely clear, while printed elements appear vibrant and professional against any background.</p>

<h3>How long do clear stickers last on windows?</h3>
<p>Our clear stickers typically last 3-5 years on exterior windows and even longer on interior applications. The high-quality transparent vinyl resists fading, cracking, and weather damage under normal conditions. Proper surface preparation and installation extend the lifespan significantly, making them suitable for both temporary and permanent installations.</p>

<h3>Can I apply clear stickers to the inside of windows for outside viewing?</h3>
<p>Yes, clear stickers work excellently for interior application with exterior visibility. Choose back-adhesive clear stickers for this application, and remember to mirror your text and design elements during the design process. This installation method protects your stickers from weather while maintaining full visibility from outside.</p>

<h3>What file format should I use for clear sticker artwork?</h3>
<p>Vector files in EPS or PDF format provide the best results for clear sticker printing. These formats ensure crisp edges and scalability without quality loss. If using raster images, provide files at 100 DPI minimum at actual size. Always convert fonts to outlines and clearly indicate cut lines for optimal production results.</p>

<h3>Do clear stickers work on curved surfaces like car windows?</h3>
<p>Clear stickers adhere well to slightly curved surfaces, but performance depends on the degree of curvature and sticker size. Smaller clear stickers conform better to curves than larger ones. For vehicles, consider die-cut options that eliminate excess material and reduce edge lifting. Test adhesion on a small area first for optimal results.</p>
