<h2>Frequently Asked Questions</h2>

<h3>Can I use my own artwork or photos for my custom photo magnets?</h3>
<p>Yes, you can upload your own photos and artwork for custom photo magnet printing. We accept high-resolution JPG, PNG, PDF, and EPS files. For best results, submit photos with at least 150 DPI at the final print size.</p>

<h3>What material are your photo magnets made from?</h3>
<p>Our photo magnets are printed on durable 30 mil magnetic material with included lamination for weather resistance. This material provides strong magnetic attraction while protecting your printed photos from moisture and UV exposure.</p>

<h3>Do your photo magnets use photo paper?</h3>
<p>No, we print directly onto magnetic material using professional digital printing processes. This creates a more durable product than traditional photo paper magnets, with better weather resistance and longer lifespan.</p>

<h3>Can I choose different cut shapes for my photo magnets?</h3>
<p>Yes, we offer rounded corners (recommended), square cut, and custom contour cutting options. Rounded corners provide better airflow and adhesion for outdoor use, while square cuts offer clean professional edges.</p>

<h3>What surfaces do magnets stick to best?</h3>
<p>Photo magnets work best on ferrous metal surfaces like refrigerators, filing cabinets, magnetic whiteboards, and steel doors. They will not stick to aluminum, plastic, or non-magnetic metal surfaces.</p>

<h3>Can photo magnets get wet?</h3>
<p>Yes, our photo magnets include lamination protection that makes them suitable for indoor and outdoor use. They can withstand moisture, light rain, and humidity without damage to the printed image.</p>

<h3>Can I write on my magnet?</h3>
<p>The laminated surface of our photo magnets is not designed for writing. If you need writable magnetic material, consider our dry erase magnets or contact customer service for custom options.</p>

<h3>How long do photo magnets last?</h3>
<p>With proper care and appropriate surface application, our photo magnets can last several years outdoors and indefinitely indoors. The lamination protection helps prevent fading and weather damage.</p>
