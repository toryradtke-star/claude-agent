<h2>Hospital Banner Artwork Requirements for Professional Printing</h2>

<p>Proper artwork preparation ensures your hospital banners print with professional quality suitable for medical facility display and healthcare organization standards.</p>

<h3>File Format Requirements</h3>
<ul>

<li>High-resolution JPEG acceptable for medical facility photography</li>

<h3>Resolution Standards for Medical Facility Banners</h3>
<p>Hospital banner artwork should be 150 DPI at actual size for optimal printing quality. Medical facility images and logos require sufficient resolution to maintain professional appearance when printed on large hospital banners.</p>

<h3>Color Specifications</h3>
<ul>
<li>CMYK color mode recommended for hospital banner artwork</li>
<li>RGB acceptable but may result in color shifts during medical facility banner printing</li>
<li>Pantone colors can be matched for medical facility brand consistency</li>
<li>Color proofs available for critical hospital banner color matching</li>
</ul>

<h3>Text and Font Guidelines</h3>
<ul>
<li>Minimum 1-inch text height for hospital banner readability from 10 feet</li>
<li>Sans-serif fonts recommended for medical facility banner clarity</li>
<li>High contrast between text and background for healthcare facility visibility</li>
<li>All fonts should be outlined or embedded in hospital banner files</li>
</ul>

<h3>Design Safety Margins</h3>
<p>Hospital banner designs should include 1-inch margins from all edges to account for hemming and finishing. Critical medical facility information should remain within safe areas to ensure complete visibility after banner finishing.</p>

<h3>Medical Facility Logo Requirements</h3>
<p>Healthcare organization logos should be vector format when possible for crisp reproduction on hospital banners. Raster medical facility logos require high resolution to maintain quality at large banner sizes.</p>
