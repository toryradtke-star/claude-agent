<h2>Insurance Banner Questions & Support</h2>

<h3>What insurance banner size works best for storefront display?</h3>
<p>For storefront insurance banner display, sizes between 3'x8' and 4'x12' provide excellent visibility while fitting most commercial building facades. Consider your building dimensions and local signage regulations when selecting size.</p>

<h3>How long do insurance banners last outdoors?</h3>
<p>Our vinyl insurance banners typically last 1-3 years outdoors depending on weather conditions, UV exposure, and care. Premium vinyl materials and UV-resistant inks extend outdoor lifespan significantly.</p>

<h3>Can you help design my insurance banner?</h3>
<p>Yes, our professional design team creates custom insurance banner designs using your company branding, messaging, and promotional requirements. Design services are available for an additional fee.</p>

<h3>Do insurance banners work for trade show displays?</h3>
<p>Insurance banners are excellent for trade show applications. Consider backdrop banner sizes (8'x10' or larger) for booth backgrounds, and smaller banners for table displays or directional signage.</p>

<h3>What's the best way to hang insurance banners?</h3>
<p>Grommeted insurance banners hang easily using rope, bungee cords, or zip ties. For windy locations, consider wind slits and secure attachment points. Banner stands provide professional indoor display options.</p>

<h3>Can I get matching insurance banners for multiple office locations?</h3>
<p>Absolutely. We can produce identical insurance banners for multiple locations with consistent branding, or customize individual banners with location-specific information while maintaining brand consistency.</p>

<h3>Do you offer rush production for insurance banners?</h3>
<p>Rush production is available for most insurance banner sizes and quantities. Same-day production may be possible for urgent needs when artwork is approved early in the business day.</p>

<h3>What file format should I use for my insurance company logo?</h3>
<p>Vector formats (AI, EPS, PDF) work best for logos to ensure sharp reproduction at any size. High-resolution PNG files with transparent backgrounds are also acceptable for most applications.</p>
