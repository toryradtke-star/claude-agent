<h2>Tennis Banner Questions & Answers</h2>

<h3>What sizes are available for tennis banners?</h3>
<p>We offer tennis banners in sizes ranging from 1'x1' up to 8'x50'. Popular sizes for tennis courts include 4'x8', 3'x6', and 2'x8' for court-side displays. Custom sizes are available for specific tournament or facility requirements.</p>

<h3>Can tennis banners withstand outdoor weather conditions?</h3>
<p>Yes, our tennis banners are made with weather-resistant vinyl material that handles outdoor tennis court conditions including sun, wind, and moisture. The fade-resistant inks maintain vibrant team colors throughout the tennis season.</p>

<h3>How long does it take to produce a custom tennis banner?</h3>
<p>Standard production time is 3-5 business days after artwork approval. Rush production options are available for urgent tournament deadlines. We recommend ordering 2-3 weeks before your event for optimal timeline planning.</p>

<h3>Do you offer design services for tennis team banners?</h3>
<p>Yes, our in-house graphic design team specializes in sports banners and can create custom designs incorporating team logos, player photos, schedules, and sponsor recognition. Design services are available at an additional cost.</p>

<h3>What finishing options work best for tennis court installation?</h3>
<p>Grommets are most popular for tennis court fence hanging, while pole pockets work well for gymnasium displays. Our customer service team can recommend the best finishing option based on your specific installation location.</p>

<h3>Can you accommodate school purchase orders for tennis banners?</h3>
<p>Yes, we accept purchase orders from schools and educational institutions. We also offer volume discounts for multiple tennis banners and can work with team parent organizations for payment coordination.</p>

<h3>Will my tennis banner arrive folded or rolled?</h3>
<p>Banners under 8 feet in both directions typically arrive rolled. Larger tennis banners will be folded due to shipping regulations. Contact us for freight shipping options if you prefer unfolded delivery for large tournament displays.</p>

<h3>What file format should I use for tennis team logos?</h3>
<p>Vector formats (AI, EPS, PDF) work best for team logos as they maintain crisp edges at any size. High-resolution JPEG or PNG files are acceptable, but should be at least 150 DPI at the final tennis banner size.</p>
