<h2>Die Cut, Kiss Cut & Transfer Cut Options for Custom Decals</h2>

<h3>Die-Cut Custom Decals</h3>
<p>Die-cut means cutting through both the vinyl decal material and the paper backing. This cut type removes all material around your design — like a cookie cutter trimming the decal to the exact contour of your artwork. Each custom decal is individually cut, leaving no extra paper or vinyl border around the design.</p>
<p><em>Note: Back Slit (Crack and Peel) is only available on Standard and Permanent material options.</em></p>

<h3>Kiss-Cut Custom Decals</h3>
<p>Kiss-cut means cutting through only the vinyl layer without cutting the paper backing. Your custom decal will have a paper material border around the design, making it easy to peel and apply. Kiss-cut is ideal for custom decals that need to be handed out or stored before application.</p>

<h3>Transfer-Cut Custom Decals</h3>
<p>Transfer-cut vinyl decals are cut to a specified shape, graphic, or text — then all excess vinyl is removed, leaving only your design on the backing. Transfer tape is applied over the remaining vinyl pieces, holding your custom decal together during application. This method lets you apply multiple individual vinyl elements cleanly and evenly onto any surface with no background showing.</p>

<h3>Transfer Cut Pick Charge Details</h3>
<p>Transfer cut pricing includes 8 standard picks with a maximum of 40 picks per square foot. Additional picks are $0.02 to $0.04 per pick depending on difficulty and intricacy. Complex custom decal designs with intricate cuts will be quoted individually based on difficulty and the time required to pick.</p>

<h2>Artwork & File Requirements for Custom Vinyl Decal Printing</h2>

<h3>Preferred Files for Printing</h3>
<p>The maximum file size accepted for custom decal orders is 100 MB. Our preferred file types are:</p>
<ul>
  <li>Adobe Illustrator EPS (Encapsulated PostScript)</li>
  <li>PDF (Adobe PDF)</li>
</ul>
<p>When submitting files for custom vinyl decal printing, convert all fonts to outlines or curves to ensure accurate reproduction.</p>

<h3>Additional Files Accepted</h3>
<ul>
  <li>JPG (High Resolution JPEG)</li>
  <li>TIFF (High Resolution TIFF)</li>
  <li>PNG (High Resolution PNG)</li>
</ul>

<h3>File Tips for Custom Decal Orders</h3>
<ul>
  <li>Set up artwork proportionate to the exact size of your custom decal order</li>
  <li>Convert all text to outlines</li>
  <li>Only allow for bleed if necessary</li>
  <li>Never flatten or merge layers in a PSD file</li>
  <li>Indicate the cut line clearly for all custom decal products</li>
</ul>

<h3>Transfer-Cut Design Guidelines</h3>
<ul>
  <li>Simple and open fonts are recommended for transfer-cut custom decals</li>
  <li>All text must be at least 1/2" tall</li>
  <li>Space between characters and design elements must be at least 1/8"</li>
  <li>Main strokes, line weights, and art elements should be a minimum of 1/8"</li>
  <li>Minimum pick size of 1/8"</li>
  <li>We recommend requesting a proof for transfer-cut decal orders</li>
</ul>

<h3>Vector Art for Decal Printing</h3>
<p>Vector art consists of mathematically defined lines and curves that can be enlarged to any size while maintaining crisp outlines and details. Vector files are ideal for custom decal printing because they reproduce logos, text, and drawn shapes at any scale without losing quality.</p>

<h3>Raster Art for Decal Printing</h3>
<p>Raster images are made up of colored pixels. Image quality depends on resolution — enlarging beyond the original size causes pixelation. For custom vinyl decal printing, follow these resolution guidelines:</p>
<ul>
  <li>100% scale (full size output) at 100 dpi</li>
  <li>50% scale at 200 dpi or higher</li>
  <li>25% scale at 300 dpi or higher</li>
  <li>10% scale at 600–1200 dpi recommended</li>
</ul>

<h3>Design Services for Custom Decals</h3>
<p>If you cannot provide a high-quality image, our in-house team offers vectorizing and artwork recreation services. Most logos or images can be recreated starting at $15, with more detailed images quoted individually. Contact a sales representative for details. Recreated vector artwork is kept on file for all future custom decal orders. Releasing the recreated file for personal use is available for an additional cost.</p>

<h2>Frequently Asked Questions About Custom Vinyl Decals</h2>

<h3>Custom Decal Product Information</h3>

<h4>What material is used for your custom vinyl decals?</h4>
<p>We offer two high-quality vinyl material options for custom decals. Our standard vinyl is made from calendered vinyl with a 3 to 5-year outdoor durability lifespan when properly applied and maintained. Our high-performance vinyl is made from cast vinyl material with up to an 8-year outdoor durability lifespan. Both materials feature air release technology for smooth, bubble-free application.</p>

<h4>Can I use multiple colors, fonts and images in my custom decal design?</h4>
<p>You can use as many as you'd like. We digitally print colors and images, so there is no extra charge for multiple colors, fonts, or images on your custom vinyl decal. Only the size, quantity, and any color matching requests affect pricing. Contact us for a color match proof.</p>

<h4>Can I upload my own design for a custom decal?</h4>
<p>Absolutely. Upload your artwork, pick your specs, and we'll print your custom decal exactly to size. We accept .eps, .pdf, and high-resolution .jpg, .tiff, and .png files.</p>

<h4>What's the difference between die cut, kiss cut, and transfer cut decals?</h4>
<p>Die cut trims your custom decal to the exact shape of your design with no extra material. Kiss cut cuts through the vinyl layer only, leaving a square paper backing for easy peeling. Transfer cut removes all background vinyl and uses transfer tape to apply only your design onto the surface — perfect for lettering and multi-piece graphics.</p>

<h4>Are custom vinyl decals waterproof?</h4>
<p>Yes. Custom decals from Decals.com are printed on outdoor-grade vinyl (standard or high-performance) and built to handle rain, sunlight, and everyday wear.</p>

<h4>Do custom decals have a gloss or matte finish?</h4>
<p>Custom vinyl decals are available in either a gloss or matte laminate finish. Choose gloss for vibrant, shiny colors or matte for a non-reflective, professional look.</p>

<h4>Can I reposition my vinyl decal after installation?</h4>
<p>We do not recommend repositioning custom decals after installation. Our adhesive is designed for a single application — reapplication will most likely result in bonding failure.</p>

<h4>How large can my custom vinyl decal be?</h4>
<p>Custom vinyl decals can be printed up to 52" high and 120" wide. For larger displays, combine multiple decal panels together. Contact us for guidance on extra-large custom decal projects.</p>

<h4>How do you remove air bubbles from custom vinyl decals?</h4>
<p>If you experience air bubbles during vinyl decal installation, check out our video guide "How To Remove An Air Bubble From Under Your Sticker" for the step-by-step process.</p>

<h3>Common Uses for Custom Vinyl Decals</h3>

<h4>What are some common uses for custom vinyl decals?</h4>
<p>Custom vinyl decals are commonly used for business logos, custom branding, and advertising products and services. Popular applications include laptop decals, car decals, custom window decals, wall decals, fleet vehicle graphics, equipment labels, and promotional giveaways.</p>

<h4>On what surfaces can custom vinyl decals be placed?</h4>
<p>Custom decals adhere best to non-porous, smooth surfaces like painted walls, metal, glass, plastic, and tile. Ensure the surface is clean and dry before application for the strongest bond.</p>

<h4>Will a custom decal leave any sticky residue behind?</h4>
<p>We use a removable adhesive on our custom decals so they can be removed cleanly. If any residue remains, a light cleaning solution will remove it.</p>

<h4>Where can I use custom vinyl decals indoors and outdoors?</h4>
<p>Custom vinyl decals can be applied both indoors and outdoors. Our outdoor-rated vinyl and UV-resistant laminate ensure your decals look great in any environment.</p>
