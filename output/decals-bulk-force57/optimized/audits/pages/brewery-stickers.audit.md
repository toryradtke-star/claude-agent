<h2>Die-Cut vs Kiss-Cut Brewery Stickers</h2>

<p>Understanding the difference between die-cut and kiss-cut options helps you choose the right brewery sticker format for your specific application. Both cutting methods offer distinct advantages depending on your brewery's needs.</p>

<h3>Die-Cut Brewery Stickers</h3>
<p>Die-cut brewery stickers are cut through both the vinyl material and paper backing, creating individually separated stickers with no excess material. This "cookie cutter" approach produces brewery stickers that match your design's exact contours. Perfect for promotional handouts, merchandise applications, and clean branded looks. Back Slit (Crack and Peel) option available on Standard and Permanent materials for easy application.</p>

<h3>Kiss-Cut Brewery Stickers</h3>
<p>Kiss-cut brewery stickers are cut only through the vinyl layer, leaving the paper backing intact around each sticker. This creates easy-to-peel brewery stickers with a paper border for simple removal and application. Ideal for <a href="https://www.decals.com/bulk-order-stickers">bulk order stickers</a> and situations where you need multiple brewery stickers on one sheet.</p>

<h3>Transfer Cut Option</h3>
<p>Transfer cut brewery stickers include a clear application tape layer that allows precise placement of multi-element designs. Perfect for complex brewery logos with multiple colors or detailed text elements that need perfect alignment during application.</p>

<h2>Brewery Sticker Artwork Requirements & File Types</h2>

<p>Proper file preparation ensures your brewery stickers print with sharp detail and accurate colors. Follow our artwork guidelines to achieve professional results that represent your brewery brand effectively.</p>

<h3>Preferred File Types</h3>
<p>Adobe Illustrator EPS (Encapsulated Post Script) and PDF (Adobe PDF) files produce the highest quality brewery stickers. Maximum file size accepted is 100 MB. When submitting these files, convert all fonts to outlines or curves to prevent font substitution issues during printing.</p>

<h3>Additional File Formats</h3>
<p>High-resolution JPG, TIFF, and PNG files are also accepted for brewery sticker printing. These raster formats work well for photographic brewery imagery but may have size limitations compared to vector files.</p>

<h3>File Setup Tips</h3>
<p>Set up artwork proportionate to your exact brewery sticker size order. Convert all text to outlines to preserve fonts. Allow for bleed only when necessary and clearly indicate cut lines for all brewery sticker products. Never flatten or merge layers in PSD files as this limits our ability to make adjustments.</p>

<h3>Vector vs Raster Art Guidelines</h3>
<p>Vector art consisting of mathematically defined lines and curves provides unlimited scalability for brewery logos and text elements. Raster images work well for brewery photography but require proper resolution: 100% scale at 100 dpi, 50% scale at 200+ dpi, or 25% scale at 300+ dpi for optimal brewery sticker print quality.</p>

<h2>How to Install Brewery Stickers</h2>

<p>Proper installation ensures your brewery stickers adhere smoothly and last longer on bottles, equipment, and promotional materials. Follow these steps for professional-looking results that enhance your brewery's brand presentation.</p>

<h3>Preparing the Surface</h3>
<p>Clean the application surface thoroughly with rubbing alcohol or soap and water to remove dirt, oils, and residue. Allow the surface to dry completely before applying brewery stickers. Cold surfaces should be warmed to room temperature for better adhesion on bottles and equipment.</p>

<h3>Applying the Brewery Sticker</h3>
<p>For die-cut brewery stickers, peel from the backing and position carefully before pressing down. Start from one edge and smooth toward the other to avoid air bubbles. For kiss-cut brewery stickers, peel each sticker from the sheet backing and apply with firm, even pressure.</p>

<h3>Removing Transfer Tape</h3>
<p>When using transfer cut brewery stickers, apply the entire design first, then slowly peel the clear transfer tape at a 45-degree angle. If any part of the brewery sticker lifts with the tape, press it back down and continue removing the transfer tape more slowly.</p>

<h3>Final Installation Tips</h3>
<p>Allow 24-48 hours for full adhesive bonding before exposing brewery stickers to moisture or temperature extremes. Small air bubbles can often be removed by pricking with a fine needle and smoothing out the air. For <a href="https://www.decals.com/domed-decals">domed decals</a> and specialty applications, contact our customer service team for specific installation guidance.</p>

<h2>Brewery Stickers FAQs</h2>

<h3>What material are brewery stickers made from?</h3>
<p>Brewery stickers are made from high-quality vinyl material that provides excellent durability and weather resistance. Our standard vinyl works well for indoor applications like merchandise and equipment labeling, while our permanent vinyl option offers enhanced durability for outdoor use and bottle applications that may be exposed to moisture or temperature changes.</p>

<h3>What surfaces work best for brewery stickers?</h3>
<p>Brewery stickers adhere well to clean, smooth surfaces including glass bottles, aluminum cans, plastic containers, metal equipment, painted walls, and most promotional materials. The surface should be clean and dry for optimal adhesion. Textured or porous surfaces may require special adhesive options.</p>

<h3>Do brewery stickers work for outdoor applications?</h3>
<p>Yes, our permanent vinyl brewery stickers are designed to withstand outdoor conditions including UV exposure, moisture, and temperature variations. These weatherproof brewery stickers maintain their color and adhesion when applied to outdoor equipment, signage, or promotional materials.</p>

<h3>What file format works best for brewery sticker artwork?</h3>
<p>Adobe Illustrator EPS files produce the highest quality brewery stickers because they maintain crisp detail at any size. PDF files with outlined fonts also work well. High-resolution JPG, PNG, and TIFF files are acceptable but may have size limitations compared to vector formats.</p>

<h3>How long do brewery stickers last on bottles?</h3>
<p>Brewery stickers typically last 3-5 years on bottles when applied properly and stored in normal conditions. Bottles exposed to frequent temperature changes or moisture may experience shorter adhesive life, but our permanent vinyl option provides enhanced durability for challenging applications.</p>

<h3>Can brewery stickers be removed without leaving residue?</h3>
<p>Our removable vinyl brewery stickers are designed to peel off cleanly from most surfaces when removed properly. For permanent applications, some adhesive residue may remain, which can be removed using rubbing alcohol or commercial adhesive remover. Heating the sticker slightly with a hair dryer makes removal easier.</p>

<h3>What cut options work best for brewery logos?</h3>
<p>Die-cut brewery stickers work best for simple logos and shapes because they create clean edges with no excess material. Kiss-cut options work well when you need multiple brewery stickers on one sheet for easy distribution. Transfer cut is ideal for complex multi-color brewery logos that require precise alignment.</p>
