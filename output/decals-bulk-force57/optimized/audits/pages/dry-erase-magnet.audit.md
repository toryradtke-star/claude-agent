<h2>Artwork Requirements for Dry Erase Magnets</h2>

<p>Proper artwork preparation ensures your custom dry erase magnets print with sharp text, vibrant colors, and professional quality. Follow our specifications to achieve the best results for your organizational magnets.</p>

<h3>Preferred File Types</h3>
<p>For optimal dry erase magnet printing quality, submit vector files in Adobe Illustrator EPS (Encapsulated PostScript) or PDF format. These file types maintain crisp edges and text at any size while ensuring accurate color reproduction. Convert all fonts to outlines or curves before submission to prevent font substitution issues.</p>

<h3>Additional File Formats</h3>
<p>We also accept high-resolution raster files including JPG, TIFF, and PNG formats for dry erase magnet projects. Raster files should be created at full size (100% scale) with a minimum resolution of 100 DPI, or proportionally higher resolution for smaller scale files.</p>

<h3>File Setup Guidelines</h3>
<p>Set up your dry erase magnet artwork at the exact proportional size you're ordering. Include bleed only when necessary, and clearly indicate cut lines for die-cut magnets. Avoid flattening or merging layers in PSD files, and ensure text is converted to outlines in vector files.</p>

<h3>Design Services</h3>
<p>If you need assistance preparing artwork for your dry erase magnets, our vectorizing and recreation services can convert low-resolution images into print-ready files. <h2>Custom Dry Erase Magnet FAQs</h2>

<h3>What material are custom dry erase magnets made from?</h3>
<p>Custom dry erase magnets are made from flexible magnetic material with a smooth, laminated surface layer. The magnetic base provides strong adhesion to metal surfaces while the laminated top layer creates a durable writing surface that works with standard dry erase markers and erases cleanly.</p>

<h3>What surfaces do dry erase magnets stick to?</h3>
<p>Dry erase magnets adhere to ferrous metal surfaces including refrigerators, filing cabinets, magnetic whiteboards, lockers, and steel doors. They will not stick to non-magnetic surfaces like aluminum, copper, stainless steel, glass, or painted walls unless those surfaces have magnetic backing.</p>

<h3>How long do dry erase magnets last?</h3>
<p>Custom dry erase magnets are designed for long-term indoor use and will maintain their magnetic strength and writing surface quality for several years. The laminated surface resists wear from repeated writing and erasing, while the magnetic backing retains its holding power through thousands of repositioning cycles.</p>

<h3>What is the largest size dry erase magnet you can print?</h3>
<p>We can produce custom dry erase magnets in various sizes to accommodate different organizational needs. Contact our customer service team to discuss specific size requirements for your project, as maximum dimensions depend on your design and intended application.</p>

<h3>Can dry erase magnets be used outdoors?</h3>
<p>Custom dry erase magnets are designed primarily for indoor use where they provide optimal performance and longevity. Outdoor exposure to weather elements may affect the magnetic strength and dry erase surface quality over time.</p>

<h3>Do you offer die-cut shapes for dry erase magnets?</h3>
<p>Yes, we offer die-cutting services that allow you to create custom-shaped dry erase magnets that follow your design outline. This option is perfect for branded organizational tools, creative classroom applications, or unique promotional magnets that stand out from standard rectangular shapes.</p>

<h3>What markers work best on dry erase magnets?</h3>
<p>Standard dry erase markers work excellently on our custom dry erase magnets and erase cleanly with a dry cloth or eraser. Avoid using permanent markers, which can stain the surface, or abrasive cleaning materials that might damage the laminated layer.</p>
