<h2>Heat Transfer Artwork & File Requirements</h2>
<p>Proper artwork preparation ensures optimal print quality and accurate color reproduction for your heat transfers. Our DTF printing process works best with high-resolution files and vector graphics.</p>

<h3>Preferred File Types</h3>
<p>Vector files provide the best results for heat transfer printing. Submit Adobe Illustrator EPS or PDF files with fonts converted to outlines. These formats maintain crisp edges and allow unlimited scaling without quality loss.</p>

<p>Maximum file size accepted is 100MB. When submitting vector files, ensure all fonts are outlined or converted to curves to prevent font substitution during processing.</p>

<h3>Additional File Formats</h3>
<p>High-resolution raster files are acceptable for photographic designs and complex graphics. Submit JPG, TIFF, or PNG files at appropriate resolution for your intended print size.</p>

<p>For full-size output, provide files at 100 DPI minimum. Smaller scale files require proportionally higher resolution: 50% scale needs 200 DPI, 25% scale requires 300 DPI, and 10% scale should be 600-1200 DPI.</p>

<h3>File Setup Tips</h3>
<p>Create artwork at the exact proportional size of your intended heat transfer. Convert all text to outlines and clearly indicate cut lines for proper processing. Avoid flattening layers in Photoshop files to maintain editing flexibility.</p>

<p>Include bleed areas only when necessary and provide specific instructions for complex designs. Our prepress team reviews all files before production to ensure optimal results.</p>

<h2>Heat Transfer Installation & Application</h2>
<p>Proper application ensures your heat transfers bond permanently to fabric and maintain durability through washing and wear. Follow these application steps for professional results.</p>

<h3>Preparing the Garment</h3>
<p>Pre-heat your garment to remove moisture and wrinkles before applying heat transfers. Ensure the fabric surface is clean and free from lint, dust, or fabric softener residue that could interfere with bonding.</p>

<p>Position your garment on a firm, flat surface or heat press platen. Place the heat transfer face-down on the desired location, ensuring proper alignment before applying heat and pressure.</p>

<h3>Applying Heat Transfers</h3>
<p>Apply firm, even pressure using a heat press or household iron at 350°F for 10-15 seconds. Use medium to heavy pressure to ensure complete bonding between the transfer and fabric fibers.</p>

<p>Allow the transfer to cool completely before removing the release liner. Peel the liner slowly at a 180-degree angle to prevent transfer damage. If any portions don't adhere properly, reapply heat and pressure.</p>

<h3>Heat Transfer Care Instructions</h3>
<p>Wait 24 hours before washing newly applied heat transfers to allow complete bonding. Wash garments inside-out in cold water using mild detergent. Avoid bleach, fabric softeners, and high-heat drying that can damage the transfer.</p>

<p>Properly applied DTF heat transfers typically last 25-50+ wash cycles when care instructions are followed. Store unused transfers in a cool, dry location away from direct sunlight.</p>

<h2>Heat Transfer FAQs</h2>

<h3>What surfaces work best for heat transfers?</h3>
<p>Heat transfers bond best to cotton, polyester, and cotton-polyester blend fabrics. They work excellently on t-shirts, hoodies, canvas bags, and other textile products that can withstand 350°F application temperature.</p>

<h3>How long do heat transfers last on garments?</h3>
<p>Properly applied heat transfers typically last 25-50+ wash cycles when care instructions are followed. Durability depends on fabric type, application technique, and washing practices.</p>

<h3>What file format works best for heat transfer artwork?</h3>
<p>Vector files like Adobe Illustrator EPS or PDF provide the best results for heat transfer printing. These formats maintain crisp edges and allow scaling without quality loss. High-resolution JPG, PNG, or TIFF files also work well for photographic designs.</p>

<h3>Can heat transfers be removed after application?</h3>
<p>Heat transfers are designed for permanent application and cannot be cleanly removed once properly bonded to fabric. Attempting removal may damage the garment or leave adhesive residue.</p>

<h3>What is the minimum order quantity for custom heat transfers?</h3>
<p>There is no minimum order quantity for custom heat transfers. You can order single transfers or large quantities depending on your project needs.</p>

<h3>Do heat transfers work with household irons?</h3>
<p>Yes, heat transfers can be applied using household irons set to high heat (cotton setting) with firm, even pressure for 10-15 seconds. Heat presses provide more consistent results but are not required.</p>

<h3>What colors are available for heat transfer printing?</h3>
<p>DTF heat transfer printing supports full-color designs including gradients, photographs, and unlimited color combinations. The printing process can reproduce virtually any color in your artwork with excellent accuracy.</p>
