<h2>Die-Cut vs Kiss-Cut Hydro Flask Stickers</h2>

<p>Understanding the difference between die-cut and kiss-cut options helps you select the best application method for your Hydro Flask stickers. The main distinction lies in how the paper backing is handled during the cutting process.</p>

<h3>Die-Cut Hydro Flask Stickers</h3>
<p>Die-cut means cutting through both the vinyl sticker material and paper backing, similar to a cookie cutter approach. This creates individually separated stickers with no excess material around each design. Your Hydro Flask stickers are precisely cut to your design's contour, providing clean edges and professional appearance.</p>

<p>Die-cut stickers work well for complex shapes and designs requiring precise boundaries. The Back Slit (Crack and Peel) feature is available on Standard and Permanent material options for easier application.</p>

<h3>Kiss-Cut Hydro Flask Stickers</h3>
<p>Kiss-cut involves cutting only through the vinyl layer while leaving the paper backing intact. This creates a paper border around your sticker design, making removal from the backing sheet simple and controlled.</p>

<p>Kiss-cut Hydro Flask stickers are ideal when you need easy peeling and application control. The extra material around each sticker provides a convenient grip area during installation on curved bottle surfaces.</p>

<p>For detailed explanations and visual examples, visit our <a href="https://www.decals.com/blog/die-cut-vs-kiss-cut">die-cut vs kiss-cut guide</a> to determine which option best suits your application needs.</p>

<h2>Hydro Flask Sticker Artwork Requirements</h2>

<p>Proper artwork preparation ensures your Hydro Flask stickers print with optimal quality and color accuracy. Follow these specifications to achieve professional results that showcase your design effectively.</p>

<h3>Preferred File Types</h3>
<p>For best printing results, submit artwork in Adobe Illustrator EPS (Encapsulated Post Script) or PDF (Adobe PDF) format. Maximum file size accepted is 100 MB. When submitting these files, convert all fonts to outlines or curves to prevent font substitution during production.</p>

<h3>Additional File Formats</h3>
<p>We also accept high-resolution JPG (JPEG), TIFF, and PNG files. These raster formats work well for photographic content and complex imagery that will be applied to Hydro Flask surfaces.</p>

<h3>File Setup Tips</h3>
<p>Set up your artwork proportionate to the exact size Hydro Flask stickers you're ordering. Convert all text elements to outlines to ensure font consistency. Allow for bleed only when necessary for full-coverage designs. Never flatten or merge layers in PSD files, and clearly indicate cut lines for all sticker products.</p>

<h3>Vector vs Raster Guidelines</h3>
<p>Vector art consists of mathematically defined lines and curves, making it ideal for logos and text that need to scale without quality loss. Raster images use colored pixels and work best for photographs. For raster files, provide artwork at 100% scale with 100 DPI resolution, or submit at 50% scale with 200+ DPI for optimal Hydro Flask sticker printing.</p>

<h2>How to Install Hydro Flask Stickers</h2>

<p>Proper installation ensures your Hydro Flask stickers adhere correctly and maintain their appearance through regular use and washing. Follow these steps for best application results on curved bottle surfaces.</p>

<h3>Preparing the Surface</h3>
<p>Clean your Hydro Flask thoroughly with soap and water to remove oils, residue, and dirt. Dry completely with a lint-free cloth. For best adhesion, allow the bottle to reach room temperature before applying stickers, especially if coming from cold or hot environments.</p>

<h3>Applying the Hydro Flask Sticker</h3>
<p>Remove the sticker from its backing material and position it carefully on the desired area. Start from one edge and smooth across the surface, working out air bubbles as you go. For curved applications, work slowly to conform the sticker to the bottle's shape without creating wrinkles.</p>

<h3>Removing Transfer Tape</h3>
<p>If your Hydro Flask stickers include transfer tape, remove it at a 45-degree angle immediately after application. Pull slowly and steadily to avoid lifting the sticker edges. Press down any areas that may have lifted during tape removal.</p>

<p>For detailed visual guidance, reference our <a href="https://www.decals.com/installation-guides">installation video guides</a> that demonstrate proper application techniques for various bottle surfaces and sticker sizes.</p>
