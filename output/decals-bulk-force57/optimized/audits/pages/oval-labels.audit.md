<h2>Die-Cut vs Kiss-Cut Oval Labels</h2>

<p>Understanding the difference between die-cut and kiss-cut options helps you choose the right style for your oval label application. The main difference lies in how the paper backing is handled during the cutting process.</p>

<h3>Die-Cut Oval Labels</h3>
<p>Die-cut means we cut completely through both the vinyl material and the paper backing, creating individual oval labels with no excess material around the design. Think of it like a cookie cutter approach - your oval labels are precisely cut to the contour of your design with clean edges and no border.</p>

<p>This style is perfect for oval labels that need to look seamless when applied, as there's no visible backing material. Die-cut oval labels work especially well for branding applications where you want the label to appear as part of the surface.</p>

<h3>Kiss-Cut Oval Labels</h3>
<p>Kiss-cut means we only cut through the vinyl layer while leaving the paper backing intact. This creates a border of paper material around each oval label, making them easier to peel and position during application.</p>

<p>Kiss-cut oval labels are ideal when you need easy handling and precise placement. The paper border provides a convenient grip area and makes the labels simple to remove from the backing sheet, especially useful for high-volume applications.</p>

<p>For detailed guidance on choosing between these options, visit our comprehensive blog article comparing <a href="https://www.decals.com/blog">die-cut vs kiss-cut labels</a>.</p>

<h2>Oval Label Artwork Requirements</h2>

<p>Proper artwork preparation ensures your oval labels print with optimal quality and color accuracy. Follow our specifications to achieve professional results with crisp graphics and vibrant colors.</p>

<h3>Preferred File Types</h3>
<p>For best print quality on oval labels, submit vector-based files whenever possible. Our preferred formats include Adobe Illustrator EPS (Encapsulated PostScript) and Adobe PDF files. When using these formats, convert all fonts to outlines or curves to prevent font substitution issues. Maximum file size accepted is 100 MB.</p>

<h3>Additional File Formats</h3>
<p>We also accept high-resolution raster files including JPG, TIFF, and PNG formats. These work well for photographic designs or when vector files aren't available for your oval label project.</p>

<h3>File Setup Guidelines</h3>
<p>Set up your artwork at the exact proportional size you're ordering for your oval labels. Convert all text to outlines to ensure fonts display correctly. Include bleed only when necessary, and never flatten or merge layers in PSD files. Clearly indicate cut lines for all oval label designs.</p>

<h3>Resolution Requirements</h3>
<p>For optimal oval label printing quality, follow these resolution guidelines: 100% scale at 100 DPI minimum, 50% scale at 200 DPI or higher, 25% scale at 300 DPI or higher, and 10% scale at 600-1200 DPI recommended for highly detailed designs.</p>

<p>Our design services team can recreate or vectorize artwork that doesn't meet these specifications. Most logos can be recreated starting at $15, with complex images quoted individually. Contact our team for details about artwork recreation services.</p>

<h2>Oval Label Installation Guide</h2>

<p>Proper installation ensures your oval labels adhere securely and maintain their appearance over time. Follow these steps for best results on any surface.</p>

<h3>Preparing the Surface</h3>
<p>Clean the application surface thoroughly with soap and water, then dry completely. Remove any dirt, oil, wax, or residue that could interfere with adhesion. For best results with oval labels, ensure the surface temperature is between 50-90°F during application.</p>

<h3>Applying Oval Labels</h3>
<p>For die-cut oval labels, carefully peel the label from its backing and position it on the desired surface. Start from one edge and smooth toward the other, working out any air bubbles with your finger or a soft squeegee.</p>

<p>Kiss-cut oval labels are easier to handle due to their paper border. Use the border as a grip area to position the label accurately before pressing down the adhesive portion.</p>

<h3>Finishing the Installation</h3>
<p>Once positioned, press firmly across the entire oval label surface to ensure complete adhesion. Pay special attention to the edges where lifting is most likely to occur. For best durability, avoid washing or exposing the area to moisture for 24 hours after application.</p>

<p>Similar to <a href="https://www.decals.com/rounded-corner-labels">rounded corner labels</a>, oval labels work best on smooth, clean surfaces like glass, metal, plastic, and painted surfaces.</p>

<h2>Oval Label FAQs</h2>

<h3>What size options are available for oval labels?</h3>
<p>Oval labels can be printed in various sizes ranging from small 1-inch labels up to large format options. Our system accommodates custom dimensions to match your specific project requirements, with both width and height fully customizable within our production capabilities.</p>

<h3>What material options work best for oval labels?</h3>
<p>We offer several vinyl materials for oval labels including standard adhesive vinyl for indoor use, permanent adhesive for long-term applications, and weather-resistant materials for outdoor durability. Each material option provides different adhesive strengths and longevity characteristics.</p>

<h3>How long do vinyl oval labels last outdoors?</h3>
<p>Quality vinyl oval labels typically last 3-5 years outdoors when applied to clean, smooth surfaces. Longevity depends on weather exposure, surface preparation, and material selection, with premium vinyl options offering extended durability in harsh conditions.</p>

<h3>Can I order oval labels in small quantities?</h3>
<p>Yes, we accommodate both small and large quantity orders for oval labels. There's no high minimum order requirement, making it cost-effective to order exactly the quantity you need for your project or business application.</p>

<h3>What surfaces work best for oval label application?</h3>
<p>Oval labels adhere best to smooth, clean surfaces including glass, metal, plastic, painted walls, and sealed wood. Textured or porous surfaces may reduce adhesion quality and longevity of the labels.</p>

<h3>Do oval labels work well for product packaging?</h3>
<p>Oval labels are excellent for product packaging, offering a distinctive shape that draws attention while providing ample space for branding, product information, and regulatory text. The curved edges also resist peeling better than sharp corners on many packaging materials.</p>

<h3>How should I prepare artwork files for oval labels?</h3>
<p>Submit artwork files in vector format (EPS or PDF) when possible, with all fonts converted to outlines. For raster images, use high resolution (300 DPI minimum) at the actual print size. Clearly indicate the desired cut line for your oval label design.</p>

<h3>Are oval labels waterproof for outdoor use?</h3>
<p>Our vinyl oval labels are water-resistant and suitable for outdoor applications. They withstand normal weather exposure including rain and humidity, though extreme conditions or submersion may affect longevity depending on the specific material selected.</p>
