<h2>Die-Cut vs Kiss-Cut Rectangle Label Options</h2>

<p>Choose between die-cut and kiss-cut options for your rectangle labels based on your application needs and installation preferences. Both cutting methods deliver professional results with different handling characteristics and backing material treatments.</p>

<h3>Die-Cut Rectangle Labels</h3>
<p>Die-cut rectangle labels are cut completely through both the vinyl label material and paper backing, creating individually separated labels with no excess material around the edges. This cutting method works like a cookie cutter, producing standalone rectangle labels that can be handled individually. Die-cut rectangle labels are ideal when you need discrete labels for direct application without peeling from backing sheets.</p>

<p>Back slit (crack and peel) backing is available on die-cut rectangle labels using Standard and Permanent material options, making removal from backing easier during installation.</p>

<h3>Kiss-Cut Rectangle Labels</h3>
<p>Kiss-cut rectangle labels are cut through the vinyl layer only, leaving the paper backing intact around each label. This creates rectangle labels that remain on a backing sheet with excess material borders for easy peeling and handling. Kiss-cut rectangle labels are perfect for applications requiring multiple labels to be stored on sheets or when installation involves peeling individual labels from backing material.</p>

<p>The extra backing material around kiss-cut rectangle labels provides convenient grip areas for removal and reduces the risk of label damage during installation.</p>

<h2>Rectangle Label Artwork Requirements</h2>

<p>Proper artwork preparation ensures your rectangle labels print with sharp graphics, accurate colors, and professional appearance. Follow our artwork guidelines to achieve optimal results for your rectangle label project.</p>

<h3>Preferred File Types</h3>
<p>Submit rectangle label artwork in Adobe Illustrator EPS (Encapsulated Post Script) or PDF (Adobe PDF) formats for best results. These vector formats maintain image quality at any size and ensure crisp text reproduction on your rectangle labels. Convert all fonts to outlines or curves before submitting to prevent font substitution issues.</p>

<p>Maximum file size accepted is 100 MB for rectangle label artwork uploads.</p>

<h3>Additional File Formats</h3>
<p>JPG (High Resolution JPEG), TIFF (High Resolution TIFF), and PNG (High Resolution PNG) files are also accepted for rectangle labels. These raster formats work well for photographic elements or detailed graphics when submitted at appropriate resolutions.</p>

<h3>File Setup Tips</h3>
<p>Set up your rectangle label artwork at exact proportional size to your ordered dimensions. Convert all text elements to outlines to ensure font compatibility. Allow for bleed only if necessary, and never flatten or merge layers in PSD files. Clearly indicate cut lines for all rectangle label designs to ensure proper trimming.</p>

<h3>Vector vs Raster Guidelines</h3>
<p>Vector artwork is ideal for rectangle labels containing text, logos, or drawn graphics because vectors maintain quality at any size. Raster images work best for photographs but require specific resolution requirements: 100% scale at 100 DPI, 50% scale at 200+ DPI, 25% scale at 300+ DPI, or 10% scale at 600-1200 DPI.</p>

<p>Our design services can recreate or vectorize existing graphics for rectangle labels starting at $15, with complex images quoted individually. Contact our team for design assistance with your rectangle label artwork.</p>

<h2>Rectangle Label Application & Installation</h2>

<p>Proper installation ensures your rectangle labels adhere securely and maintain professional appearance throughout their lifespan. Follow these application steps for optimal rectangle label performance on various surfaces.</p>

<h3>Preparing the Surface</h3>
<p>Clean the application surface thoroughly with isopropyl alcohol or appropriate cleaner to remove dirt, oil, and residue. Allow the surface to dry completely before applying rectangle labels. Smooth, non-porous surfaces like glass, metal, and plastic provide the best adhesion for rectangle labels.</p>

<h3>Applying Rectangle Labels</h3>
<p>Remove rectangle labels from backing material carefully to avoid stretching or damaging the vinyl. Position the label accurately before contact - vinyl adhesive bonds immediately upon contact. Apply firm, even pressure across the entire rectangle label surface, working from center outward to eliminate air bubbles.</p>

<p>For larger rectangle labels, use a squeegee or credit card wrapped in soft cloth to ensure complete adhesion and smooth application.</p>

<h3>Installation Tips</h3>
<p>Room temperature installation provides best results for rectangle labels. Cold temperatures can make vinyl less flexible, while excessive heat may cause premature adhesive activation. Allow rectangle labels to cure for 24-48 hours before exposure to moisture or extreme conditions.</p>
