Domed Decal Materials & Construction

Our domed decals are manufactured using premium materials and specialized processes to deliver exceptional durability and visual appeal. Understanding the construction helps you select the right application method for your specific needs.

Die-Cut Construction

Domed decals are produced as die-cut decals with precise edge cutting that follows your design contours. This construction method provides clean edges and professional appearance while allowing the polyurethane dome to extend to the design perimeter.

The die-cutting process occurs after the doming application, ensuring that both the base material and polyurethane coating are cut cleanly in a single operation. This prevents edge separation and maintains the weatherproof seal.

Sheet Format Supply

Custom domed decals are supplied in 10" x 12.5" sheet format rather than individual pieces or roll format. This sheet format protects the raised dome surface during shipping and provides convenient handling for application.

The sheet format allows multiple domed decals to be produced efficiently while maintaining the precision required for the specialized doming process. Individual decals are separated from the sheet during the final die-cutting stage.

Custom Domed Decal Artwork Requirements

Proper artwork preparation is essential for achieving optimal results with domed decals. The specialized printing and coating processes require specific file formats and setup guidelines to ensure crisp reproduction and proper dome application.

Preferred File Types

EPS, AI, PSD, and high-resolution PDF files provide the best results for domed decal production. High-resolution JPG and TIF files are also acceptable for photographic elements. GIF and PUB files cannot be processed due to format limitations.

File Setup Requirements

All fonts must be converted to outlines to prevent substitution issues during production. Set up artwork at proportionate size to your ordered dimensions, and provide non-photographic elements in vector format when possible. Photographic images require 300 DPI resolution for crisp reproduction under the polyurethane dome.

Clearly indicate cut lines for die-cutting accuracy, and specify PMS colors when color matching is critical. Include all linked graphics and avoid flattening PSD layers to maintain editing flexibility during production setup.

Design Services

Domed Decal Application & Installation

Proper application technique ensures maximum adhesion and longevity for your domed decals. The raised dome surface requires specific handling methods to achieve professional results without damaging the polyurethane coating.

Preparing the Surface

Clean the application surface thoroughly with isopropyl alcohol to remove dirt, oils, and residue. Allow the surface to dry completely before beginning application. Smooth, non-porous surfaces like metal, glass, and hard plastics provide optimal adhesion for domed decals.

Applying the Domed Decal

Remove the backing paper carefully to avoid touching the adhesive. Position the domed decal precisely before initial contact, as repositioning may damage the dome or reduce adhesive strength. Apply firm, even pressure around the perimeter and base areas, avoiding excessive pressure on the raised dome center.

Final Installation Steps

Work out air bubbles by pressing from the center toward the edges, using light pressure to avoid dome damage. For rounded corner labels and similar applications, ensure complete edge adhesion to maintain the weatherproof seal that protects the underlying adhesive.

Allow 24-48 hours for full adhesive cure before exposing domed decals to moisture, temperature extremes, or mechanical stress. This curing period ensures maximum bond strength and long-term durability.

Domed Decal FAQs

What materials are used in domed decals?

Domed decals are constructed with a screenprinted base layer and a crystal clear polyurethane top coat that creates the characteristic raised dome appearance. The polyurethane coating provides waterproof, weatherproof, and chemical-resistant properties while enhancing color vibrancy and adding dimensional depth to the printed design.

How long do domed decals last outdoors?

Domed decals typically last 5-7 years in outdoor applications due to their protective polyurethane coating. The dome provides superior resistance to UV fading, scratches, chemicals, salt, acid, and oil exposure, with self-healing properties that help recover from minor surface damage automatically.

What surfaces work best for domed decal application?

Smooth, clean, non-porous surfaces like metal, glass, and hard plastics provide optimal adhesion for domed decals. The strong adhesive bonds permanently to properly prepared surfaces while the polyurethane dome maintains flexibility to accommodate minor surface variations without cracking.

Can domed decals be customized to specific shapes and sizes?

Yes, domed decals are die-cut to follow your custom design contours and can be produced in various sizes within our production capabilities. The die-cutting process occurs after dome application to ensure clean edges and proper polyurethane coating coverage to the design perimeter.

What file format works best for domed decal artwork?

EPS files provide the best results for domed decal artwork because they maintain vector quality and color accuracy during the specialized screening and coating process. High-resolution PDF, AI, and PSD files are also acceptable, while all fonts should be converted to outlines for consistent reproduction.

How should domed decals be stored before application?

Store domed decals in their original sheet format in a cool, dry location away from direct sunlight until application. The sheet format protects the raised dome surfaces from damage while maintaining adhesive integrity. Avoid stacking heavy objects on top of the sheets to prevent dome compression.

Are domed decals removable without surface damage?

Domed decals are designed for permanent application and may be difficult to remove completely due to their strong adhesive bond. While removal is possible with heat application and careful techniques, some adhesive residue may remain, and the process may affect certain surface finishes, particularly vehicle paint.
