Cut Options for Label Sheets

Label sheets are available with different cutting methods to match your application preferences and removal requirements. Each cutting style affects how the labels separate from the backing and from each other.

Die Cut Label Sheets

Die cut label sheets feature labels that are cut completely through both the vinyl and backing material. This creates individual labels that can be easily peeled away from the surrounding material, perfect for precise application on products or packages.

Kiss Cut Label Sheets

Kiss cut label sheets have labels cut through the vinyl layer only, leaving the backing paper intact. This allows labels to be peeled off individually while keeping the sheet structure stable, ideal for high-volume labeling tasks.

Perforated Label Sheets

Perforated label sheets include scored lines between label sections for clean, straight tears. This option works well when you need to separate portions of the sheet or create tear-away sections for different uses.

Artwork Requirements for Label Sheets

Proper artwork setup ensures your label sheets print clearly and cut accurately. Follow these guidelines to achieve professional results with your custom label sheet design.

Preferred File Types

Vector files (.AI, .EPS, .PDF) work best for label sheet artwork because they scale without losing quality and provide clean edges for cutting. These formats ensure your text and graphics remain sharp at any label size.

Additional File Formats

High-resolution raster files (.PNG, .JPG, .PSD) are also accepted for label sheet designs. Ensure images are at least 300 DPI at the final label size to maintain print quality across all labels on the sheet.

File Setup Tips

Include cut lines or guides in your artwork to show exactly where each label should be positioned and cut. Leave adequate spacing between labels to ensure clean separation and professional appearance.

Color and Font Guidelines

Use CMYK color mode for accurate print reproduction on label sheets. Outline all fonts or include font files with your artwork to prevent text substitution issues during production.

Label Sheet Application & Installation

Proper application technique ensures your label sheets adhere securely and maintain their professional appearance. Follow these steps for best results when applying labels from your custom sheet.

Preparing the Surface

Clean the application surface thoroughly with rubbing alcohol or mild detergent to remove dirt, oil, and residue. Allow the surface to dry completely before applying any labels from the sheet. Smooth surfaces like glass, plastic, and metal provide the strongest adhesion.

Applying Individual Labels

For kiss cut label sheets, carefully peel each label from the backing paper starting at one corner. Position the label accurately before making contact with the surface, as repositioning may reduce adhesive strength. Press firmly from the center outward to eliminate air bubbles.

Working with Die Cut Labels

Die cut labels can be removed from the sheet by gently pushing up from behind or using the "weeding" method to remove excess material. Take care not to stretch the vinyl during removal to maintain the label's shape and dimensions.

What Are Label Sheets?

Label sheets are collections of individual labels arranged and printed on a single sheet of material, typically vinyl with an adhesive backing. These sheets contain multiple labels in organized rows and columns, making them efficient for businesses that need to apply numerous labels quickly. Label sheets are commonly used for product labeling, packaging identification, inventory management, and organizational systems. The labels can be kiss cut (cut through the face material only) or die cut (cut completely through the material and backing) depending on the application requirements and removal preferences.

Custom Label Sheet FAQs

How long do label sheets last when applied outdoors?

Most vinyl label sheets last 3-5 years outdoors when applied to clean, smooth surfaces like glass or metal. Exposure to direct sunlight and weather conditions may gradually fade colors, but the adhesive typically maintains its bond throughout the lifespan.

What surfaces work best for label sheet application?

Label sheets adhere best to smooth, non-porous surfaces such as glass, plastic, metal, and coated paper. Textured or porous surfaces like untreated wood or fabric may not provide reliable adhesion for long-term applications.

What file format should artwork be for label sheets?

Vector files (.AI, .EPS, .PDF) are preferred for label sheet artwork because they provide clean, scalable graphics and text. High-resolution raster files (.PNG, .JPG) at 300 DPI minimum are also acceptable for photographic elements.

What is the difference between kiss cut and die cut label sheets?

Kiss cut label sheets have labels cut through the face material only, leaving the backing intact for easy peeling. Die cut label sheets are cut completely through both the material and backing, allowing labels to be removed individually from the surrounding material.

Can label sheets be customized with different label sizes on one sheet?

Yes, label sheets can accommodate different label sizes and shapes on a single sheet. This flexibility allows you to create mixed-size labels for various products or applications while maintaining efficient sheet utilization.

How should label sheets be stored before application?

Store label sheets flat in a cool, dry environment away from direct sunlight and extreme temperatures. Keep sheets in their protective packaging until ready for use to prevent dust accumulation and maintain adhesive quality.

What is the minimum order quantity for custom label sheets?

Custom label sheets can be ordered in small quantities starting from single sheets, making them cost-effective for both small businesses and large-scale operations. Bulk order stickers and labels offer additional cost savings for high-volume needs.
