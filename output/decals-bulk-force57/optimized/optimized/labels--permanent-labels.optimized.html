Cut Options for Permanent Labels

Choose between die-cut and kiss-cut options based on your application needs. Each cutting method offers distinct advantages for different permanent label applications and removal preferences.

Die-Cut Permanent Labels

Die-cut permanent labels are cut completely through both the vinyl material and paper backing, creating individually separated labels with no surrounding material. This "cookie cutter" approach produces labels that follow the exact contour of your design, providing a clean, professional appearance ideal for product branding and equipment identification.

Kiss-Cut Permanent Labels

Kiss-cut permanent labels are cut only through the vinyl layer while leaving the paper backing intact. This creates a border of backing material around each label, making removal from the backing sheet easier during application. The surrounding material provides a convenient grip for positioning labels accurately.

Back Slit Option

Available for die-cut permanent labels, the back slit feature adds a crack-and-peel functionality to the backing paper. This option simplifies label removal and application, particularly useful for high-volume labeling tasks or when wearing gloves.

Permanent Label Artwork Requirements

Proper artwork preparation ensures your permanent labels print with crisp detail and accurate colors. Follow our specifications to achieve optimal results for your custom labeling project.

Preferred File Types

For highest quality permanent labels, submit vector-based files in Adobe Illustrator EPS (Encapsulated PostScript) or PDF format. These file types maintain sharp edges and clean text at any size. Maximum file size is 100 MB. Convert all fonts to outlines or curves to prevent font substitution during printing.

Additional File Formats

We accept high-resolution raster files including JPG, TIFF, and PNG formats. For raster artwork, provide files at 100% scale with minimum 100 DPI resolution. Higher resolution files (300+ DPI) produce better results, especially for detailed graphics or small text.

File Setup Tips

Set up your artwork proportionate to your ordered permanent label size. Convert all text to outlines to ensure accurate font reproduction. Include bleed only when necessary for full-coverage designs. Clearly indicate cut lines for custom shapes, and avoid flattening or merging layers in PSD files to maintain editing flexibility.

Design Services

If you need artwork creation or enhancement, our design team can recreate logos and graphics starting at $15 for simple designs. Complex artwork may require custom quoting. This service requires advance payment and includes file storage for future permanent label orders. Contact our team to discuss your design needs and obtain pricing for artwork services.

Applying Your Permanent Labels

Proper application technique ensures maximum adhesion and longevity for your permanent labels. Follow these steps for professional results that will last through regular use and environmental exposure.

Preparing the Surface

Clean the application surface thoroughly with isopropyl alcohol or degreasing cleaner to remove dirt, oils, and residue. Allow the surface to dry completely before applying permanent labels. Ensure the surface temperature is between 50-90°F for optimal adhesive activation. Smooth, non-porous surfaces like metal, glass, and hard plastics provide the strongest bond.

Applying the Permanent Label

Remove the label from its backing carefully, avoiding contact with the adhesive. Position the label accurately before contact, as the strong adhesive makes repositioning difficult. Apply pressure from the center outward to eliminate air bubbles and ensure complete adhesive contact. Use a squeegee or credit card for larger labels to achieve uniform pressure.

Final Adhesion

Allow 24-48 hours for the permanent adhesive to reach full strength. Avoid exposure to moisture or extreme temperatures during this curing period. Once fully adhered, permanent labels create a strong bond that resists removal, making them ideal for equipment identification, asset tracking, and outdoor applications where durability is essential.

What Are Permanent Labels?

Permanent labels are adhesive labels manufactured with high-strength acrylic adhesive designed to create a lasting bond with application surfaces. Made from durable vinyl material, these labels resist weathering, UV exposure, and regular wear while maintaining their appearance and adhesion strength. Common applications include equipment identification, asset tagging, product labeling, and outdoor signage where long-term durability is required. Installation involves cleaning the surface and applying firm pressure to activate the permanent adhesive bond.

Permanent Label FAQs

What surfaces work best for permanent labels?

Permanent labels adhere strongest to clean, smooth, non-porous surfaces including metal, glass, hard plastics, and painted surfaces. Clean surfaces with alcohol or degreaser before application for maximum adhesion strength.

How long do permanent labels last outdoors?

Our permanent labels typically last 5-7 years outdoors when properly applied to suitable surfaces. UV-resistant vinyl and fade-resistant inks help maintain appearance and adhesion through weather exposure and sunlight.

What file format works best for permanent label artwork?

Vector files in Adobe Illustrator EPS or PDF format produce the highest quality permanent labels. These formats maintain sharp edges and clean text at any size while ensuring accurate color reproduction during printing.

Can permanent labels be removed after application?

Permanent labels are designed for long-term application and are difficult to remove without damage to the label or surface. Removal typically requires heat application and adhesive remover, and may leave residue on the surface.

What is the minimum quantity for permanent label orders?

We accommodate both small and large permanent label orders with no minimum quantity requirements. Pricing scales based on quantity, size, and production options selected during the ordering process.

Do you provide proofs before printing permanent labels?

Yes, we email a digital proof for approval before production begins on your permanent labels. This ensures your design appears correctly and meets your expectations before manufacturing starts.

What cutting options are available for permanent labels?

Choose between die-cut permanent labels (cut through material and backing) or kiss-cut labels (cut through material only, leaving backing intact). Die-cut provides individual labels while kiss-cut includes backing material for easier handling.
