Roll Label Artwork Requirements

Proper artwork setup ensures your roll labels print with optimal quality and color accuracy. Follow these guidelines to achieve professional results with your custom roll label designs.

Preferred File Types

Vector files (AI, EPS, PDF) produce the highest quality roll labels with crisp text and clean graphics. These formats maintain quality at any size and ensure sharp printing results.

High-Resolution Raster Files

When vector files aren't available, submit high-resolution raster images (PNG, JPG, TIFF) at 300 DPI minimum. Ensure images are sized appropriately for your final roll label dimensions to prevent pixelation.

Color Considerations

Design your roll labels using CMYK color mode for print accuracy. RGB colors may appear differently when printed. For waterproof roll labels on clear material, ensure sufficient contrast between text and background elements for optimal readability.

Text and Font Guidelines

Convert all fonts to outlines or curves before submitting artwork. Use font sizes of 8pt or larger to ensure legibility on your finished roll labels. Consider how text will appear on your chosen material - white text works well on clear labels over dark products.

Roll Label Installation Guide

Proper application ensures your roll labels adhere securely and maintain professional appearance throughout their intended lifespan. Follow these steps for optimal results with both waterproof and paper roll labels.

Surface Preparation

Clean application surfaces thoroughly with alcohol or degreasing solution to remove dirt, oils, and residue. Ensure surfaces are completely dry before applying roll labels. Smooth, non-porous surfaces like glass, plastic, and metal provide the best adhesion.

Label Application Process

Remove roll labels from backing material carefully to avoid stretching or damage. Position labels accurately before initial contact - repositioning may reduce adhesive strength. Apply firm, even pressure from center outward to eliminate air bubbles and ensure complete contact.

Achieving Professional Results

For curved surfaces like bottles, apply roll labels gradually while maintaining tension to prevent wrinkles. Use a squeegee or credit card to smooth labels during application. Allow 24 hours for full adhesive cure, especially for waterproof roll labels in challenging environments.

Temperature Considerations

Apply roll labels at room temperature for best results. Cold temperatures may reduce initial adhesive tack, while excessive heat can make labels difficult to position. Store unused roll labels in moderate temperature conditions to maintain adhesive performance.

Common applications include product branding, ingredient labeling, promotional messaging, and package identification across industries like food and beverage, retail, manufacturing, and e-commerce.

Roll Label FAQs

What surfaces work best for roll labels?

Roll labels adhere best to smooth, non-porous surfaces like glass, plastic, metal, aluminum, and coated cardboard. These surfaces provide optimal adhesion for both waterproof BOPP and paper roll labels, ensuring long-lasting application.

How long do waterproof roll labels last outdoors?

Waterproof BOPP roll labels typically last 3-5 years outdoors when applied to appropriate surfaces. Their resistance to moisture, oils, and UV exposure makes them suitable for products stored in challenging environments or exposed to weather conditions.

What is the difference between waterproof and paper roll labels?

Waterproof roll labels use synthetic BOPP material that resists moisture, chemicals, and food acids, making them ideal for beverage and food applications. Paper roll labels offer economical printing for short-term or high-volume applications where moisture resistance isn't critical.

Can I get roll labels in custom shapes?

Standard roll labels are available in circle, square, rectangle, and oval shapes. For unique shapes beyond these options, consider our shape cut labels which offer more complex die-cutting capabilities for specialized branding needs.

What file format should my roll label artwork be?

Vector files (AI, EPS, PDF) produce the highest quality roll labels with crisp text and graphics. High-resolution raster files (PNG, JPG) at 300 DPI minimum are acceptable alternatives when vector files aren't available.

Do roll labels work on curved surfaces like bottles?

Yes, roll labels conform well to curved surfaces when applied properly. Waterproof BOPP material offers excellent flexibility for bottle applications, while proper application technique ensures smooth, wrinkle-free results on cylindrical containers.

How are roll labels packaged for shipping?

Roll labels ship on their backing material in protective packaging to prevent damage during transit. Labels remain flat and ready for application, with rigid packaging ensuring they arrive in perfect condition for immediate use.
