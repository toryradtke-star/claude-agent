Die-Cut vs Kiss-Cut Rounded Corner Labels

Choose between two cutting methods that affect how your rounded corner labels separate from their backing material. Each cutting option provides different handling characteristics and application methods to suit various use cases and preferences.

Die-Cut Rounded Corner Labels

Die-cutting cuts completely through both the vinyl label material and paper backing, creating individually separated rounded corner labels with no excess material. This cutting method works like a cookie cutter, producing labels that are ready to peel and apply directly. Die-cut rounded corner labels arrive as individual pieces without paper borders, making them ideal for quick application and storage efficiency.

This option works best when you need labels that are easy to handle individually and don't require the stability of surrounding backing material. Back slit (crack and peel) functionality is available on standard and permanent material options for even easier application.

Kiss-Cut Rounded Corner Labels

Kiss-cutting cuts only through the vinyl layer while leaving the paper backing intact, creating rounded corner labels with paper material borders around each label. This method provides extra material around your labels for easy peeling and precise placement during application.

Kiss-cut rounded corner labels remain attached to their backing sheet until you peel them off, which helps with organization and provides stability during handling. The paper border makes it easier to position labels accurately before final application.

Rounded Corner Label Artwork Requirements

Proper file preparation ensures your rounded corner labels print with optimal quality and accurate color reproduction. Follow our artwork specifications to achieve professional results that showcase your design with crisp detail and vibrant colors.

Preferred File Types

Submit vector-based files for the highest quality rounded corner labels. Adobe Illustrator EPS (Encapsulated Post Script) and PDF formats provide the best results for text and graphics that remain sharp at any size. Convert all fonts to outlines or curves before submission to prevent font substitution issues. Maximum file size is 100 MB.

Additional File Formats

Raster files are accepted when vector formats aren't available. Submit JPG, TIFF, or PNG files at high resolution with proper scaling. Use 100% scale at 100 DPI, 50% scale at 200+ DPI, 25% scale at 300+ DPI, or 10% scale at 600-1200 DPI for optimal results.

File Setup Guidelines

Set up your artwork proportionate to your ordered rounded corner label size. Convert all text to outlines to maintain font integrity. Include bleed only when necessary and indicate cut lines clearly for proper production. Avoid flattening or merging layers in PSD files to maintain editing flexibility.

How to Apply Rounded Corner Labels

Proper application ensures your rounded corner labels adhere securely and maintain their professional appearance over time. Follow these installation steps for best results on various surfaces and conditions.

Preparing the Surface

Clean the application surface thoroughly before applying rounded corner labels. Remove dirt, grease, and residue using appropriate cleaners for the surface material. Allow surfaces to dry completely before label application. Smooth, non-porous surfaces provide the best adhesion for rounded corner labels.

Applying Rounded Corner Labels

Peel labels carefully from their backing material, starting at one corner. Position the rounded corner label on your target surface and press firmly from the center outward to eliminate air bubbles. The rounded edges make positioning easier and reduce the risk of corner lifting that can occur with sharp-edged labels.

Finishing Application

Apply consistent pressure across the entire rounded corner label surface to ensure complete adhesion. Pay special attention to the rounded edges to secure proper bonding. Allow labels to set for several hours before exposing them to moisture or extreme temperatures for optimal performance.

For best results, apply rounded corner labels at room temperature and avoid application in direct sunlight or high humidity conditions. The vinyl material and adhesive perform optimally under normal environmental conditions.

Rounded Corner Labels FAQs

What materials are used to make rounded corner labels?

Rounded corner labels are made from high-quality vinyl material that provides durability and weather resistance. The vinyl construction allows for vibrant color reproduction and maintains its appearance over time when applied to appropriate surfaces.

How small or large can rounded corner labels be made?

Rounded corner labels can be produced in various sizes to accommodate different applications. Contact our customer service team to discuss specific size requirements and ensure your desired dimensions work with our production capabilities and maintain the rounded corner aesthetic.

What surfaces work best for rounded corner labels?

Rounded corner labels adhere best to smooth, clean, non-porous surfaces such as glass, metal, plastic, and painted surfaces. The curved edges reduce the likelihood of corner lifting that can occur with traditional sharp-edged labels on textured surfaces.

Do rounded corner labels have glossy or matte finishes available?

Rounded corner labels are available in different finish options depending on your aesthetic preferences and application requirements. The finish affects both the appearance and durability characteristics of your labels.

What's the difference between die-cut and kiss-cut rounded corner labels?

Die-cut rounded corner labels are individually separated with no backing material borders, while kiss-cut labels remain on backing sheets with paper borders around each label. Die-cut labels offer easier individual handling, while kiss-cut labels provide more control during application.

Can I get samples of rounded corner labels before placing a large order?

How long do rounded corner labels last when applied outdoors?

The outdoor durability of rounded corner labels depends on the specific vinyl material, environmental conditions, and surface preparation. Properly applied labels on suitable surfaces typically maintain their appearance and adhesion for several years under normal outdoor exposure.
