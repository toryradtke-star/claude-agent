Travel Sticker Cut Options & Backing Choices

Understanding cut options helps you select the best travel sticker format for your application method and aesthetic preferences. Each cutting method affects how your travel stickers separate from the backing material.

Die Cut Travel Stickers

Die-cut travel stickers are cut through both the vinyl material and paper backing, creating individual stickers with no excess material. This "cookie cutter" approach produces travel stickers that follow your design's exact contours, perfect for logos, custom shapes, and detailed travel artwork.

Back Slit (Crack and Peel) options are available with Standard and Permanent materials, making application easier for larger travel stickers on luggage and gear.

Kiss Cut Travel Stickers

Kiss-cut travel stickers are cut only through the vinyl layer, leaving the paper backing intact around your design. This creates a paper border around each travel sticker, making them easy to peel and apply. Kiss-cut is ideal for bulk order stickers and applications where quick removal from sheets is important.

Transfer Cut Travel Stickers

Transfer cut combines precision cutting with application tape, allowing complex multi-part designs to be applied as a single unit. This option works well for detailed travel logos and multi-color designs that need precise alignment on travel gear.

Travel Sticker Artwork Requirements & File Specifications

Proper file preparation ensures your travel stickers print with sharp details and accurate colors. Follow these specifications to achieve professional results for your custom travel sticker designs.

Preferred File Types

For optimal travel sticker quality, submit Adobe Illustrator EPS (Encapsulated PostScript) or PDF files. These vector formats maintain crisp lines and text at any size. Convert all fonts to outlines or curves to prevent font substitution issues. Maximum file size is 100 MB.

Additional File Formats

High-resolution JPG, TIFF, and PNG files are acceptable for photographic travel images or when vector files aren't available. Ensure sufficient resolution for your travel sticker size to avoid pixelation in the final product.

File Setup Tips

Set up artwork proportionate to your ordered travel sticker dimensions. Convert all text to outlines before submission. Include bleed only when necessary for your design. For layered files, avoid flattening or merging layers. Clearly indicate cut lines for die-cut travel stickers.

Vector vs. Raster Guidelines

Vector artwork uses mathematical curves and is ideal for logos, text, and geometric travel designs. Vector files can be scaled without quality loss, perfect for travel stickers that might be produced in multiple sizes. Raster images consist of pixels and work well for photographic travel imagery but have scaling limitations.

Travel Sticker Installation & Application Guide

Proper application ensures your travel stickers adhere securely and maintain their appearance throughout your adventures. Follow these steps for professional-looking results on luggage, water bottles, and travel gear.

Preparing the Surface

Clean the application surface thoroughly with rubbing alcohol or degreasing cleaner. Remove all dirt, oils, and residue that could prevent proper adhesion. Allow the surface to dry completely before applying travel stickers. Smooth, non-porous surfaces like metal, plastic, and glass provide the best adhesion.

Applying the Travel Sticker

Remove the backing paper and position the travel sticker carefully - adhesive bonds immediately upon contact. For larger travel stickers, use the hinge method: tape one edge, remove backing, and smooth progressively across the surface. Work out air bubbles using a squeegee or credit card.

Removing Transfer Tape

If your travel stickers include transfer tape, remove it at a 45-degree angle immediately after application. Pull slowly and steadily to ensure the vinyl remains adhered to the surface rather than lifting with the transfer tape.

Travel Sticker FAQs

What materials are used for travel stickers?

Travel stickers are made from durable vinyl material with UV-resistant, rain-resistant, and scratch-resistant laminate coating. This construction ensures travel stickers maintain their appearance and adhesion through exposure to weather, handling, and travel conditions while remaining flexible enough for application on various surfaces.

How long do travel stickers last on luggage?

Travel stickers typically last 3-5 years on luggage when properly applied to clean surfaces. The protective laminate coating helps resist fading from UV exposure and protects against scratches from handling and transport, making them ideal for frequent travelers who need durable identification and personalization.

What surfaces work best for travel sticker application?

Travel stickers adhere best to smooth, non-porous surfaces including hard plastic luggage, metal water bottles, glass, and painted surfaces. They also work well on smooth fabric luggage and leather goods. Textured or heavily curved surfaces may require careful application to ensure proper adhesion.

Can travel stickers be removed without damaging luggage?

Yes, travel stickers can typically be removed from most surfaces without damage when removed properly. Gentle heat from a hair dryer can soften the adhesive for easier removal. Any adhesive residue can usually be cleaned with adhesive remover or rubbing alcohol depending on the surface material.

What cut options work best for travel stickers?

Die-cut travel stickers work well for logo shapes and custom designs since they follow the exact contour of your artwork with no excess material. Kiss-cut options are ideal when you want easy removal from sheets or prefer a uniform border around your design for consistent appearance.

Do you offer waterproof travel stickers?

Yes, our travel stickers feature water-resistant vinyl material with protective laminate that prevents water penetration and moisture damage. This makes them suitable for outdoor gear, water bottles, and luggage that may be exposed to rain or humid conditions during travel.

What file format should I use for travel sticker artwork?

For best results, submit vector files in Adobe Illustrator EPS or PDF format with fonts converted to outlines. These formats ensure crisp printing at any size and prevent font substitution issues. High-resolution JPG, PNG, or TIFF files are acceptable for photographic travel images.
