<h2>Artwork Requirements for Magnetic Business Cards</h2>
<p>Proper artwork preparation ensures your magnetic business cards print with crisp text, vibrant colors, and precise registration. We accept various file formats, though vector-based files produce the highest quality results for magnetic business card printing.</p>

<h3>Preferred File Types</h3>
<p>Adobe Illustrator EPS (Encapsulated PostScript) and Adobe PDF files provide optimal print quality for magnetic business cards. When submitting these formats, convert all fonts to outlines or curves to prevent font substitution issues. Maximum file size is 100 MB to ensure efficient processing.</p>

<h3>Additional File Formats</h3>
<p>High-resolution JPEG, TIFF, and PNG files are acceptable for magnetic business card orders. These raster formats work best when created at full size with sufficient resolution: 300 DPI at actual size, 600 DPI at 50% scale, or 1200 DPI at 25% scale.</p>

<h3>File Setup Guidelines</h3>
<p>Set up your artwork at the exact dimensions of your magnetic business cards to ensure proper proportions. Convert all text to outlines to prevent font issues, and clearly indicate cut lines for die-cut options. Include bleed areas only when extending graphics to the edges, and never flatten layers in Photoshop files.</p>

<h2>Installation & Application of Magnetic Business Cards</h2>
<p>Magnetic business cards are designed for easy application and repositioning on compatible metal surfaces. Proper placement ensures maximum adhesion and longevity for your magnetic business cards.</p>

<h3>Preparing the Surface</h3>
<p>Clean the target metal surface with a dry cloth to remove dust, oil, and debris that could interfere with magnetic adhesion. Smooth, flat metal surfaces provide the strongest hold for magnetic business cards. Textured or painted metal surfaces may reduce magnetic strength.</p>

<h3>Applying Magnetic Business Cards</h3>
<p>Place your magnetic business card against the cleaned metal surface and press firmly to ensure full contact. The flexible magnetic material will conform to slight surface variations. Magnetic business cards can be repositioned multiple times without losing adhesive strength.</p>

<h3>Compatible Surfaces</h3>
<p>Magnetic business cards adhere best to ferrous metals including steel, iron, and magnetic stainless steel. They will not stick to aluminum, brass, copper, or non-magnetic stainless steel surfaces. Test adhesion on a small area before applying multiple magnetic business cards. <h2>Magnetic Business Card FAQs</h2>

<h3>What material are magnetic business cards made from?</h3>
<p>Magnetic business cards are manufactured using flexible rubber magnetic material with full-color digital printing and a protective laminate finish. This construction provides durability, weather resistance, and strong magnetic adhesion to compatible metal surfaces.</p>

<h3>What surfaces work best for magnetic business cards?</h3>
<p>Magnetic business cards adhere strongly to ferrous metals including steel, iron, and some stainless steel surfaces. They work perfectly on refrigerators, filing cabinets, metal doors, and office equipment. They will not stick to aluminum, brass, copper, or non-magnetic materials.</p>

<h3>What are the standard sizes for magnetic business cards?</h3>
<p>Standard magnetic business card dimensions are 3.5" x 2" and 2" x 3.5" to match traditional business card proportions. Custom sizes are available to accommodate unique design requirements or specific magnetic business card applications.</p>

<h3>How long do magnetic business cards last outdoors?</h3>
<p>Magnetic business cards feature weather-resistant laminate coating that protects against moisture, UV rays, and temperature changes. With proper application on suitable metal surfaces, magnetic business cards typically maintain their appearance and magnetic strength for 2-3 years in outdoor conditions.</p>

<h3>Can magnetic business cards be die-cut into custom shapes?</h3>
<p>Yes, magnetic business cards can be die-cut into virtually any shape or configuration. Custom die-cutting requires vector artwork with clearly marked cut lines and may involve additional setup costs depending on complexity. Standard shapes and rounded corners are included at no extra charge.</p>

<h3>What file format works best for magnetic business card artwork?</h3>
<p>Adobe Illustrator EPS and PDF files produce the highest quality results for magnetic business card printing. High-resolution JPEG, TIFF, and PNG files are also acceptable. Convert all fonts to outlines and ensure artwork is created at actual size with 300 DPI resolution for optimal print quality.</p>
