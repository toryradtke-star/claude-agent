<h2>Die-Cut, Kiss-Cut & Transfer-Cut Equipment Decals</h2>
<p>Choose the cutting method that best suits your equipment decal application. Each cut type offers different backing paper behavior and application characteristics for various equipment marking needs.</p>

<h3>Die-Cut Equipment Decals</h3>
<p>Die-cut equipment decals are cut completely through both the vinyl and backing paper, creating individually shaped decals with no excess material. Like a cookie cutter, your equipment decals are precisely cut to match your design contour. Perfect for equipment serial numbers, company logos, and warning labels that need clean, professional edges. Back slit (crack and peel) feature available on standard and permanent materials for easier application on curved equipment surfaces.</p>

<h3>Kiss-Cut Equipment Decals</h3>
<p>Kiss-cut equipment decals are cut through only the vinyl layer, leaving the backing paper intact around your design. This creates a border of backing material that makes equipment decals easier to handle and position during application. Ideal for equipment decals that will be applied to flat machinery surfaces or when you need extra material for gripping during installation.</p>

<h3>Transfer-Cut Equipment Decals</h3>
<p>Transfer-cut equipment decals involve cutting vinyl to specific shapes, then removing unwanted material and applying transfer tape over the remaining design. This method works best for detailed equipment lettering, intricate logos, and multi-piece designs. The transfer tape holds all pieces together for clean application of complex equipment identification graphics. Requires vector files (EPS or Adobe Illustrator format). Transfer cutting includes 8 standard picks with up to 40 picks per square foot. Additional picks charged $0.02-$0.04 per pick based on complexity.</p>

<h2>Equipment Decal Artwork Requirements & File Setup</h2>

<h3>Preferred File Types</h3>
<p>For the best equipment decal printing results, submit Adobe Illustrator EPS (Encapsulated Post Script) or PDF files. Maximum file size is 100 MB. When submitting these files for equipment decal production, convert all fonts to outlines or curves to ensure accurate printing and prevent font substitution that could affect equipment identification text.</p>

<h3>Additional File Formats</h3>
<p>We also accept high-resolution JPG, TIFF, and PNG files for equipment decal printing. These formats work well for photographic equipment decals or designs that don't require vector precision. Ensure sufficient resolution for clear printing at your intended equipment decal size.</p>

<h3>File Setup Tips</h3>
<p>Set up artwork files proportionate to your exact equipment decal order size. Convert all text to outlines to prevent font issues during equipment decal production. Allow for bleed only when necessary for full-coverage designs. Never flatten or merge layers in PSD files. Clearly indicate cut lines for all equipment decal products to ensure proper manufacturing.</p>

<h3>Transfer Cut Guidelines</h3>
<p>Transfer-cut equipment decals require special file preparation. Use simple, open fonts for equipment lettering. All text must be at least 1/2" tall for reliable cutting. Maintain 1/8" minimum spacing between characters and design elements. Main strokes and line weights should be at least 1/8" wide. Minimum pick size is 1/8". We recommend requesting a proof for complex transfer-cut equipment decals to ensure design feasibility.</p>

<h2>Equipment Decal Installation & Application Guide</h2>
<p>Proper installation ensures your equipment decals adhere correctly and last longer in demanding industrial environments. Follow these steps for professional equipment decal application on machinery, tools, and equipment surfaces.</p>

<h3>Preparing the Surface</h3>
<p>Clean the equipment surface thoroughly with isopropyl alcohol or degreasing solution to remove dirt, oil, and residue. Allow the surface to dry completely before applying equipment decals. Smooth, non-porous surfaces like painted metal, plastic housings, and glass work best for equipment decal adhesion. Avoid applying decals to heavily textured, dirty, or freshly painted equipment surfaces.</p>

<h3>Applying the Equipment Decal</h3>
<p>Position your equipment decal carefully before removing the backing paper. For larger equipment decals, remove backing paper gradually while smoothing the decal onto the surface to prevent air bubbles. Use a squeegee or application tool to ensure complete contact between the decal and equipment surface. Work from the center outward to eliminate trapped air.</p>

<h3>Transfer Tape Removal</h3>
<p>For transfer-cut equipment decals, wait a few minutes after application before slowly removing the transfer tape at a 45-degree angle. If any decal pieces lift with the transfer tape, press them back down and wait longer before attempting removal. Properly applied transfer-cut equipment decals will remain on the surface when transfer tape is removed.</p>

<h2>Equipment Decal FAQs</h2>

<h3>What material is used for your equipment decals?</h3>
<p>Our equipment decals are made from high-quality vinyl material designed for durability in industrial environments. The vinyl resists fading, cracking, and weather damage, making it ideal for equipment exposed to outdoor conditions, moisture, and temperature changes.</p>

<h3>What types of surfaces can equipment decals stick to?</h3>
<p>Equipment decals adhere best to clean, smooth surfaces like painted metal, plastic equipment housings, glass, and non-porous materials. They work well on machinery, tools, vehicle equipment, and most industrial surfaces when properly prepared and applied.</p>

<h3>Are these equipment decals weatherproof?</h3>
<p>Yes, our equipment decals are designed to withstand outdoor conditions including rain, sun exposure, and temperature fluctuations. The weatherproof vinyl construction ensures your equipment identification remains visible and intact in demanding work environments.</p>

<h3>How long do equipment decals last on machinery?</h3>
<p>Equipment decals typically last 3-5 years on outdoor machinery and equipment when applied to clean, appropriate surfaces. Indoor equipment decals can last even longer, with the actual lifespan depending on environmental conditions and surface preparation quality.</p>

<h3>Can I remove equipment decals without damaging the surface?</h3>
<p>Equipment decals can be removed from most surfaces, though some adhesive residue may remain depending on how long the decal was applied and environmental exposure. Use heat and adhesive remover for easier removal from valuable equipment surfaces.</p>

<h3>What cut option works best for equipment serial numbers?</h3>
<p>Die-cut equipment decals work excellently for serial numbers and identification text, providing clean edges without excess material. Kiss-cut decals offer easier handling for serial number applications, while transfer-cut works best for detailed equipment lettering and logos.</p>

<h3>Do you offer bulk pricing for large equipment decal orders?</h3>
<p>Yes, we provide volume discounts for large equipment decal orders. Contact our customer service team for bulk pricing quotes when ordering equipment decals for entire machinery fleets or facility-wide equipment marking projects.</p>
