<h2>Photo Magnet Artwork Requirements</h2>

<p>Achieve outstanding print quality for your photo magnets by following our artwork specifications. Proper file preparation ensures your photos reproduce with sharp detail, accurate colors, and professional results that showcase your images beautifully.</p>

<h3>Preferred File Types for Photo Magnets</h3>
<p>For optimal photo magnet printing, submit Adobe Illustrator EPS (Encapsulated Post Script) or PDF (Adobe PDF) files with a maximum size of 100 MB. When using these vector formats, convert all fonts to outlines or curves to prevent font substitution and ensure accurate text reproduction on your photo magnets.</p>

<h3>Additional File Formats</h3>
<p>We also accept high-resolution JPG (JPEG), TIFF, and PNG files for photo magnet production. These raster formats work well for photographic content and should meet specific resolution requirements to achieve professional print quality.</p>

<h3>Photo Magnet File Setup Tips</h3>
<p>Set up your artwork proportionate to your exact photo magnet order size for accurate scaling. Convert all text elements to outlines to preserve font appearance and allow for bleed only when necessary. Never flatten or merge layers in PSD files, and clearly indicate cut lines for any custom-shaped photo magnets.</p>

<h3>Resolution Guidelines for Photo Magnets</h3>
<p>High-resolution files ensure your photo magnets print with exceptional clarity. Submit files at 100% scale with 100 dpi minimum, 50% scale at 200+ dpi, 25% scale at 300+ dpi, or 10% scale at 600-1200 dpi for optimal results. Low-resolution files may result in pixelation that compromises photo quality on your finished magnets.</p>

<h2>Photo Magnet Installation & Application</h2>

<p>Applying your custom photo magnets is straightforward and requires no special tools or adhesives. The magnetic backing provides instant, repositionable attachment to any ferrous metal surface, making installation and removal simple and damage-free.</p>

<h3>Preparing the Magnetic Surface</h3>
<p>Clean the target surface with a damp cloth to remove dust, grease, or debris that might interfere with magnetic adhesion. Photo magnets work best on smooth, flat metal surfaces such as refrigerators, filing cabinets, whiteboards with steel backing, and magnetic bulletin boards. Ensure the surface is completely dry before applying your photo magnets.</p>

<h3>Applying Your Photo Magnets</h3>
<p>Simply place your photo magnet against the prepared metal surface and press gently to ensure full contact. The magnetic backing will automatically secure the magnet in position. Photo magnets can be easily repositioned or removed without leaving residue or causing surface damage, making them perfect for temporary or changeable displays.</p>

<h3>Maximizing Photo Magnet Performance</h3>
<p>For strongest magnetic hold, ensure the entire back surface of your photo magnet makes contact with the metal surface. Avoid placing magnets over paint chips, rust, or uneven surfaces that might create air gaps. Store unused photo magnets flat and away from extreme temperatures to maintain magnetic strength and prevent photo damage over time.</p>

<h2>Photo Magnet FAQs</h2>

<h3>What magnetic materials are used to make photo magnets?</h3>
<p>Photo magnets are made from flexible magnetic vinyl material that provides strong adhesion to ferrous metal surfaces. All photo magnets include a protective laminate finish that guards against moisture, scratches, and UV fading while maintaining vibrant photo quality over extended use.</p>

<h3>What surfaces do photo magnets stick to?</h3>
<p>Photo magnets adhere to any ferrous metal surface including refrigerators, filing cabinets, steel doors, magnetic whiteboards, and metal bulletin boards. They do not stick to aluminum, brass, copper, or non-metal surfaces, so verify your target surface contains iron or steel before ordering.</p>

<h3>Can photo magnets get wet without damage?</h3>
<p>Yes, photo magnets feature a waterproof laminate coating that protects the printed image from moisture exposure. The magnetic backing is also moisture-resistant, making these magnets suitable for kitchen use, bathroom applications, and other areas where occasional water contact may occur.</p>

<h3>Is there a minimum order quantity for photo magnets?</h3>
<p>No minimum order quantity is required for photo magnets. You can order as few as one custom photo magnet or as many as needed for your project, with pricing that accommodates both small personal orders and large commercial quantities.</p>

<h3>Can photo magnets be cut to match my photo's shape?</h3>
<p>Yes, we offer die-cut photo magnets that follow the exact contour of your image or design. This precision cutting removes excess magnetic material around your photo, creating a custom shape that perfectly highlights your image while maintaining strong magnetic performance.</p>

<h3>Do black and white photo magnets cost less than color versions?</h3>
<p>Photo magnet pricing is based on size and quantity rather than color content. Black and white photos print at the same cost as full-color images, allowing you to choose the aesthetic that best suits your design without price considerations affecting your decision.</p>

<h3>What are creative ways to use custom photo magnets?</h3>
<p>Photo magnets serve many creative purposes including family photo displays, business promotional giveaways, real estate marketing tools, event announcements, product showcase magnets, and personalized gifts. They're also popular for organizing spaces with labeled photo magnets that identify contents or locations while adding visual interest.</p>
