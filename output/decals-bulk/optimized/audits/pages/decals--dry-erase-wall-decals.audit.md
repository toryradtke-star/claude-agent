<h3>File Setup Tips</h3>
<p>Create artwork proportionate to your exact dry erase wall decal size and convert all text to outlines or curves. Indicate cut lines clearly and avoid flattening layers in PSD files. Include bleed only when necessary and provide clear instructions for any special requirements.</p>

<h2>Dry Erase Wall Decal Installation Guide</h2>

<p>Proper installation ensures your dry erase wall decals adhere smoothly and perform optimally. Follow these step-by-step instructions for professional results that will provide years of reliable whiteboard functionality.</p>

<h3>Preparing the Surface</h3>
<p>Clean the installation area thoroughly with mild soap and water, removing all dirt, dust, and residue. The surface should be completely dry before applying your dry erase wall decal. Smooth, non-porous surfaces like painted walls, glass, and metal provide the best adhesion.</p>

<h3>Applying the Decal</h3>
<p>Position your dry erase wall decal carefully before removing the backing paper, as repositioning becomes more difficult once adhesive contact is made. Apply from one edge using a squeegee or credit card to smooth out air bubbles, working systematically across the entire surface.</p>

<h3>Removing Transfer Tape</h3>
<p>If your dry erase wall decal includes transfer tape, remove it slowly at a 45-degree angle after the decal is fully applied. If any part of the decal lifts with the tape, press it back down and continue removal more slowly.</p>

<h2>Dry Erase Wall Decal FAQs</h2>

<h3>Can I reposition my dry erase wall decal during installation?</h3>
<p>Yes, dry erase wall decals can be repositioned during initial installation before full adhesive contact is made. Once the decal is fully applied and smoothed down, repositioning becomes more difficult and may affect adhesive strength.</p>

<h3>What surfaces work best for dry erase wall decals?</h3>
<p>Dry erase wall decals adhere best to smooth, non-porous surfaces like painted walls, glass, metal, and laminated surfaces. Textured walls or porous materials may not provide adequate adhesion for long-term use.</p>

<h3>What file format should I use for my dry erase wall decal artwork?</h3>
<p>Adobe Illustrator EPS files with fonts converted to outlines provide the best print quality for dry erase wall decals. PDF files are also acceptable, while high-resolution JPG, TIFF, and PNG files work for photographic designs.</p>

<h3>How large can I make my dry erase wall decal?</h3>
<p>Dry erase wall decals can be produced in virtually any size up to our maximum width capabilities. Large decals may be printed in sections for easier installation, with seams carefully planned to minimize visibility.</p>

<h3>Will dry erase wall decals damage my paint when removed?</h3>
<p>Properly applied dry erase wall decals should remove cleanly from most painted surfaces without damaging the paint. However, removal results can vary depending on paint type, age, and application method.</p>

<h3>What markers work with dry erase wall decals?</h3>
<p>Standard dry erase markers work perfectly with our dry erase wall decals. The smooth vinyl surface allows easy writing and complete erasing without ghosting or staining when proper dry erase markers are used.</p>

<h3>How do I remove air bubbles from dry erase wall decals?</h3>
<p>Small air bubbles can be smoothed out using a squeegee or credit card, working from the center outward. For persistent bubbles, carefully prick with a fine needle and smooth out the air.</p>
