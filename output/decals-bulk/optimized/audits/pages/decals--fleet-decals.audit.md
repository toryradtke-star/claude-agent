<h2>Fleet Decals Cut Options & Material Formats</h2>

<p>Fleet decals are available in three professional cut options that determine how the vinyl material and backing paper are processed. Each cutting method offers specific advantages for different vehicle application needs and design requirements.</p>

<h3>Die-Cut Fleet Decals</h3>
<p>Die-cut processing cuts completely through both the vinyl fleet decal material and paper backing, creating individually cut graphics with no excess material around the design. This "cookie cutter" approach produces clean, professional fleet graphics that follow the exact contour of your design. Back slit option is available on standard and permanent material selections for easier application.</p>

<h3>Kiss-Cut Fleet Decals</h3>
<p>Kiss-cut fleet decals are cut only through the vinyl layer while leaving the paper backing intact, creating a border of backing material around each graphic. This cutting method provides extra material for easy handling and positioning during fleet vehicle installation.</p>

<h3>Transfer-Cut Fleet Decals</h3>
<p>Transfer-cut fleet decals involve cutting the vinyl to specified shapes and removing unwanted material, leaving only the desired design elements. Transfer tape is applied over the remaining vinyl pieces to hold multi-part designs together during application. This method works exceptionally well for fleet lettering and complex logo applications. Vector files in EPS or AI format are required for transfer-cut fleet graphics.</p>

<p>Transfer-cut pricing includes up to 8 standard picks per square foot, with additional picks charged separately based on complexity. Intricate fleet decal designs may require custom quoting depending on the detail level and <a href="https://www.decals.com/stickers/crack-and-peel-stickers">crack and peel stickers</a> production requirements.</p>

<h2>Fleet Decals Artwork Requirements & File Specifications</h2>

<p>Professional fleet decals require proper artwork preparation to ensure optimal printing quality and accurate reproduction of your vehicle graphics. Follow these specifications to achieve the best results for your fleet branding project.</p>

<h3>Preferred File Types</h3>
<p>Adobe Illustrator EPS files and PDF formats provide the highest quality for fleet decals printing. When submitting these files, convert all fonts to outlines or curves to prevent font substitution issues. Maximum file size accepted is 100 MB.</p>

<h3>Additional File Formats</h3>
<p>High-resolution JPEG, TIFF, and PNG files are acceptable for fleet decals when vector files are unavailable. Raster images should follow specific resolution guidelines: 100% scale at 100 DPI, 50% scale at 200 DPI or higher, 25% scale at 300 DPI or higher, or 10% scale at 600-1200 DPI for optimal results.</p>

<h3>File Setup Tips</h3>
<p>Set up artwork proportionate to your exact fleet decals order size. Convert all text elements to outlines, allow for bleed only when necessary, and never flatten or merge layers in PSD files. Clearly indicate cut lines for all decal products to ensure proper manufacturing.</p>

<h3>Transfer-Cut Guidelines</h3>
<p>Fleet decals using transfer-cut methods require specific design considerations. Use simple, open fonts with text at least 1/2" tall. Maintain minimum 1/8" spacing between characters and design elements. Main strokes and line weights should be at least 1/8" with minimum pick size of 1/8". Vector artwork is essential for transfer-cut fleet graphics.</p>

<p>If your artwork requires enhancement, our design services can recreate or vectorize images starting at $15, with complex graphics subject to custom quoting. We maintain recreated vector files for future fleet decal orders and can provide artwork files for personal use at additional cost.</p>

<h2>Fleet Decals Installation Guide & Application Tips</h2>

<p>Proper fleet decals installation ensures professional appearance and maximum adhesion longevity on vehicle surfaces. Follow these step-by-step instructions for successful fleet graphics application.</p>

<h3>Preparing the Surface</h3>
<p>Clean the vehicle surface thoroughly with soap and water, then use isopropyl alcohol to remove any remaining residue, wax, or contaminants. The surface must be completely dry before applying fleet decals. Optimal application temperature is between 50-90°F for best adhesive performance.</p>

<h3>Applying the Fleet Decals</h3>
<p>Position fleet decals carefully before removing the backing paper. For larger graphics, use the hinge method by taping one edge and folding the decal back to remove backing paper gradually. Use a squeegee or application tool to smooth the vinyl from center outward, eliminating air bubbles.</p>

<h3>Removing Transfer Tape</h3>
<p>For transfer-cut fleet decals, remove the transfer tape slowly at a 45-degree angle while the vinyl is still slightly warm. If any vinyl lifts with the transfer tape, press it back down and continue removal more slowly. Allow 24-48 hours for full adhesive cure before washing or heavy weather exposure.</p>

<p>Professional fleet decals installation may require specialized tools for large graphics or complex vehicle curves. Consider professional installation for extensive fleet branding projects to ensure optimal results and warranty compliance.</p>

<h2>Fleet Decals FAQs</h2>

<h3>How long do fleet decals last on vehicles?</h3>
<p>Fleet decals typically last 5-7 years on vehicles when applied to clean surfaces and manufactured with quality vinyl materials. Our weatherproof fleet graphics resist fading, cracking, and peeling under normal outdoor conditions, providing long-term value for business vehicle branding.</p>

<h3>What surfaces work best for fleet decals application?</h3>
<p>Fleet decals adhere best to smooth, clean surfaces like painted metal, fiberglass, and glass. Avoid textured surfaces, fresh paint (less than 30 days old), or surfaces treated with wax or protective coatings. Proper surface preparation is essential for optimal fleet graphics adhesion.</p>

<h3>Can fleet decals be removed without damaging vehicle paint?</h3>
<p>Quality fleet decals can be removed cleanly when applied correctly and removed properly using heat and appropriate techniques. Removal is easier within the first 3-5 years of application. Professional removal may be recommended for large fleet graphics or aged installations.</p>

<h3>What file format works best for fleet decals artwork?</h3>
<p>Adobe Illustrator EPS files provide the best quality for fleet decals printing, especially for logos and text-based designs. Vector formats ensure crisp reproduction at any size. High-resolution raster files (JPEG, PNG, TIFF) are acceptable when vector artwork is unavailable.</p>

<h3>Are rush production options available for fleet decals?</h3>
<p>Yes, fleet decals can be produced in as little as one business day with our priority production option. Express production (2 days), standard production (3 days), and saver production (5 days) are also available to match your fleet branding timeline requirements.</p>

<h3>What cut option should I choose for my fleet decals?</h3>
<p>Die-cut works well for simple logos and shapes, kiss-cut provides extra handling material for positioning, and transfer-cut is ideal for multi-color text and complex designs. The best cut option depends on your fleet graphics design complexity and application preferences.</p>
