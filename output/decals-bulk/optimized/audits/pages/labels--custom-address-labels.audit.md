<h2>Address Label Artwork Requirements & File Specifications</h2>

<h3>Preferred File Types</h3>
<p>For optimal address label quality, submit Adobe Illustrator EPS (Encapsulated Post Script) or PDF files. The maximum file size accepted is 100 MB. When submitting these files, convert all fonts to outlines or curves to ensure accurate printing and prevent font substitution on your address labels.</p>

<h3>Additional File Formats</h3>
<p>We also accept high-resolution JPG, TIFF, and PNG files for address label production. These raster formats work well for photographic elements or complex designs that don't require vector precision.</p>

<h3>File Setup Tips</h3>
<p>Set up your address label artwork at the exact proportional size you're ordering. Convert all text elements to outlines to maintain font integrity. Include bleed only when necessary for full-coverage designs. Never flatten or merge layers in PSD files, and clearly indicate cut lines for all address label designs.</p>

<h3>Vector vs. Raster Guidelines</h3>
<p>Vector artwork scales to any size while maintaining crisp details, making it ideal for address label text and simple graphics. Raster images work best for photographs but require proper resolution: 100 DPI at full size, 200 DPI at 50% scale, or 300+ DPI at 25% scale for quality address label reproduction.</p>

<p>If you need design assistance, our vectorizing service can recreate logos and images for as little as $15. Contact our team for address label design support and file preparation guidance.</p>

<h2>Address Label Installation & Application Guide</h2>

<p>Proper application ensures your address labels adhere securely and maintain professional appearance throughout handling and mailing. Follow these steps for best results with your custom address labels.</p>

<h3>Preparing the Surface</h3>
<p>Clean the envelope, package, or surface where your address labels will be applied. Remove any dust, moisture, or residue that could interfere with adhesion. Address labels adhere best to smooth, clean surfaces at room temperature.</p>

<h3>Applying Address Labels</h3>
<p>Peel your address label from its backing carefully, starting from one corner. Position the label accurately before making contact - repositioning may reduce adhesive strength. Apply firm, even pressure from the center outward to eliminate air bubbles and ensure complete adhesion.</p>

<h3>Final Application Tips</h3>
<p>Allow address labels to set for several minutes before handling or mailing. Avoid applying address labels in extremely cold or humid conditions, as this can affect adhesive performance. For best results, apply address labels at least 24 hours before mailing when possible.</p>

<p>Address labels work best on paper envelopes, cardboard packages, and other clean, dry surfaces. Avoid textured or heavily treated surfaces that may interfere with proper adhesion.</p>

<h2>Address Label FAQs</h2>

<h3>What material is used for custom address labels?</h3>
<p>Our address labels are made from durable vinyl material with strong adhesive backing. This vinyl construction resists moisture, handling, and normal wear during mailing, ensuring your address information remains clearly visible and securely attached throughout the delivery process.</p>

<h3>Do address labels come on sheets or rolls?</h3>
<p>Address labels are typically provided on sheets for easy storage and organization. Sheet format allows you to peel individual labels as needed while keeping unused address labels protected on their backing material. Roll format may be available for larger quantities - contact us to discuss your specific address label requirements.</p>

<h3>What size options are available for personalized address labels?</h3>
<p>Address labels are available in multiple standard sizes to fit different envelope types and design requirements. Common sizes include small rectangular formats for standard envelopes and larger sizes for packages or return address applications. Custom sizing is available to match your specific address label needs.</p>

<h3>Do address labels have glossy or matte finish options?</h3>
<p>Address labels can be produced with either glossy or matte finishes depending on your preference. Glossy finish provides vibrant colors and professional appearance, while matte finish offers reduced glare and a more subtle look. Both finishes provide excellent durability for mailing applications.</p>

<h3>Will multiple colors and fonts impact address label pricing?</h3>
<p>No, the number of colors and fonts in your address label design does not affect pricing. Our digital printing process handles complex, multi-color designs at the same cost as simple single-color address labels. You can include as many design elements as needed without additional charges.</p>

<h3>What surfaces work best for custom address labels?</h3>
<p>Address labels adhere best to clean, smooth surfaces like paper envelopes, cardboard boxes, and similar mailing materials. Avoid applying address labels to heavily textured, dusty, or treated surfaces that may interfere with proper adhesion and long-term performance.</p>

<h3>How long do address labels maintain adhesion during shipping?</h3>
<p>Our address labels are designed to maintain strong adhesion throughout standard shipping and handling processes.
