<h2>Wedding Label Cut Options & Backing Types</h2>

<p>Wedding labels are available in two distinct cutting methods, each offering different application benefits depending on your specific use case and design preferences.</p>

<h3>Die Cut Wedding Labels</h3>
<p>Die-cut wedding labels are cut completely through both the vinyl label material and paper backing, similar to a cookie cutter approach. This cutting method creates individually separated labels with no excess material around each design. Die-cut wedding labels work exceptionally well when you want clean, precise label edges that follow your design contour exactly. Back slit (crack and peel) functionality is available for standard and permanent material options, making application easier for multiple wedding labels.</p>

<h3>Kiss Cut Wedding Labels</h3>
<p>Kiss-cut wedding labels are cut only through the vinyl layer while leaving the paper backing intact. This creates a paper border around each wedding label, making them easier to peel and position during application. Kiss-cut wedding labels are ideal when you're applying many labels in sequence, as the backing provides a convenient grip area and prevents accidental label damage during handling.</p>

<p>Consider your application method and event timeline when selecting between die-cut and kiss-cut options for your wedding labels. Both cutting methods deliver professional results with the same high-quality vinyl materials and printing specifications.</p>

<h2>Wedding Label Artwork Requirements & File Specifications</h2>

<p>Proper artwork preparation ensures your wedding labels print with optimal quality and color accuracy. Understanding file requirements helps achieve professional results that enhance your wedding aesthetic.</p>

<h3>Preferred File Types</h3>
<p>For best wedding label quality, submit Adobe Illustrator EPS (Encapsulated Post Script) or Adobe PDF files with a maximum file size of 100 MB. When using these vector formats, convert all fonts to outlines or curves to prevent font substitution during production.</p>

<h3>Additional File Formats</h3>
<p>High-resolution JPG, TIFF, and PNG files are also accepted for wedding label artwork. These raster formats work well for photographic elements or complex designs that don't require vector scalability.</p>

<h3>File Setup Guidelines</h3>
<p>Set up your wedding label artwork at the exact proportional size you're ordering. Convert all text elements to outlines, allow for bleed only when necessary, and clearly indicate cut lines for all wedding label designs. For Photoshop files, never flatten or merge layers as this limits production flexibility.</p>

<h3>Resolution Requirements</h3>
<p>Raster images should follow these resolution guidelines: 100% scale at 100 dpi minimum, 50% scale at 200+ dpi, 25% scale at 300+ dpi, or 10% scale at 600-1200 dpi for optimal wedding label output quality.</p>

<p>Our design services can recreate or vectorize existing artwork for as little as $15, with complex designs subject to custom quoting. This service requires advance payment and your recreated artwork remains on file for future wedding label orders.</p>

<h2>Wedding Label Application & Installation Guide</h2>

<p>Proper wedding label application ensures professional appearance and reliable adhesion throughout your wedding celebration. Following these application techniques helps achieve smooth, bubble-free results on various surfaces.</p>

<h3>Surface Preparation</h3>
<p>Clean all surfaces thoroughly before applying wedding labels. Remove dust, oils, and residue using a lint-free cloth and appropriate cleaning solution. Allow surfaces to dry completely before wedding label application. Smooth, non-porous surfaces like glass, metal, and high-quality paper provide optimal adhesion for wedding labels.</p>

<h3>Application Technique</h3>
<p>Start wedding label application from one edge, gradually smoothing toward the opposite edge to eliminate air bubbles. For larger wedding labels, use a squeegee or <a href="https://www.decals.com/stickers/crack-and-peel-stickers">application tool</a> to ensure even pressure distribution. Work slowly to maintain control and prevent wrinkles or misalignment.</p>

<h3>Temperature Considerations</h3>
<p>Apply wedding labels at room temperature when possible. Extreme cold can make adhesive less effective, while excessive heat may cause premature adhesion before proper positioning. Allow applied wedding labels to cure for 24-48 hours before exposure to moisture or handling stress.</p>

<p>For wedding labels applied to textured or curved surfaces, additional pressure and longer cure time may be necessary. Test application technique on a small area first when working with delicate materials or antique surfaces for your wedding decorations.</p>

<h2>Wedding Label FAQs</h2>

<h3>What file format works best for wedding label artwork?</h3>
<p>Adobe Illustrator EPS or PDF files provide the best quality for wedding labels. These vector formats ensure crisp text and graphics at any size, with a maximum file size limit of 100 MB accepted.</p>

<h3>How long do wedding labels take to produce?</h3>

<h3>What's the difference between die-cut and kiss-cut wedding labels?</h3>
<p>Die-cut wedding labels are cut completely through the material and backing, creating individual labels with no excess material. Kiss-cut wedding labels are cut only through the vinyl layer, leaving paper backing for easier peeling and application.</p>

<h3>What surfaces work best for wedding label application?</h3>
<p>Wedding labels adhere best to clean, smooth surfaces like glass, metal, high-quality paper, and sealed wood. Textured or porous surfaces may require additional pressure and longer cure time for optimal adhesion.</p>

<h3>Can I get a proof before my wedding labels are printed?</h3>

<h3>What sizes are available for custom wedding labels?</h3>
<p>Wedding labels are available in sizes ranging from 2" to 12" in both height and width dimensions. Custom size quotes are available for dimensions outside this standard range.</p>

<h3>Do wedding labels work for outdoor wedding receptions?</h3>
<p>Yes, our vinyl wedding labels are suitable for outdoor use and resist fading from sun exposure. However, for extended outdoor exposure or extreme weather conditions, consider discussing material options with our customer service team.</p>
