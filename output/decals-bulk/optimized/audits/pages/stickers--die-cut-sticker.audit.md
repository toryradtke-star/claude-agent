<h2>Die-Cut Sticker Artwork Requirements</h2>

<p>Proper artwork preparation ensures your die-cut stickers print with sharp colors and precise cut lines. Our artwork guidelines help you submit files that produce professional results while avoiding common formatting issues that can delay production.</p>

<h3>Preferred File Types</h3>
<p>For optimal die-cut sticker quality, submit artwork in Adobe Illustrator EPS (Encapsulated Post Script) or PDF format. These vector-based formats maintain crisp edges at any size and provide the most accurate cut line definition for precision die-cutting.</p>

<p>When submitting EPS or PDF files, convert all fonts to outlines or curves to prevent font substitution during production. Maximum file size accepted is 100 MB for all die-cut sticker artwork submissions.</p>

<h3>Additional File Formats</h3>
<p>We also accept high-resolution JPG, TIFF, and PNG files for die-cut sticker production. These raster formats work well for photographic designs but require proper resolution to maintain quality during printing and cutting.</p>

<h3>File Setup Guidelines</h3>
<p>Set up your artwork at the exact proportional size you're ordering to ensure accurate scaling. Clearly indicate cut lines for all die-cut sticker designs, and convert all text to outlines before submission. Allow for bleed only if necessary, and avoid flattening or merging layers in Photoshop files.</p>

<h3>Vector vs. Raster Artwork</h3>
<p>Vector artwork uses mathematically defined lines and curves that can be scaled to any size while maintaining crisp edges - ideal for die-cut stickers with text and logo elements. Raster images consist of pixels and work best for photographic content, but require sufficient resolution to prevent pixelation during printing.</p>

<h2>Die-Cut Sticker Installation Guide</h2>

<p>Proper installation technique ensures your die-cut stickers adhere smoothly and maintain their appearance over time. Following these installation steps helps prevent air bubbles, wrinkles, and premature peeling for professional-looking results.</p>

<h3>Preparing the Surface</h3>
<p>Clean the application surface thoroughly with a mild detergent solution and allow it to dry completely before applying your die-cut stickers. Remove any dirt, grease, or residue that could interfere with adhesion. The surface should be smooth, dry, and at room temperature for optimal adhesion.</p>

<h3>Applying Die-Cut Stickers</h3>
<p>Remove the die-cut sticker from its backing paper carefully, starting from one corner. For larger stickers, remove the backing gradually while smoothing the sticker onto the surface to prevent air bubbles. Use firm pressure across the entire sticker surface to ensure proper adhesion.</p>

<p>Work slowly and methodically, especially with detailed die-cut designs. If air bubbles appear, use a credit card or squeegee to smooth them toward the nearest edge. Small pin holes can help release stubborn air bubbles without damaging the sticker.</p>

<h3>Final Installation Steps</h3>
<p>After applying your die-cut sticker, run your finger or a smooth tool around all edges to ensure complete adhesion. Allow the adhesive to set for at least 24 hours before exposing the sticker to moisture or extreme temperatures for maximum bond strength.</p>

<p>For outdoor applications, consider the weather conditions during installation. Avoid applying die-cut stickers in direct sunlight or extreme temperatures, as this can affect the adhesive performance and final appearance.</p>

<h2>Die-Cut Sticker FAQs</h2>

<h3>What material is used for die-cut stickers?</h3>
<p>Our die-cut stickers are made from premium vinyl material that provides excellent durability and color retention. The vinyl material is weather-resistant and suitable for both indoor and outdoor applications, with adhesive backing that bonds well to most clean, smooth surfaces.</p>

<h3>How are die-cut stickers different from regular stickers?</h3>
<p>Die-cut stickers are precision-cut through both the vinyl and backing material to follow your design's exact shape, eliminating excess background material. Regular stickers typically have rectangular or square backing that extends beyond the design, while die-cut stickers create individually separated pieces that match your artwork's contour perfectly.</p>

<h3>What file format works best for die-cut sticker artwork?</h3>
<p>Adobe Illustrator EPS files work best for die-cut stickers because they maintain crisp edges and clear cut lines at any size. PDF files with outlined fonts are also excellent choices. High-resolution JPG, PNG, and TIFF files are acceptable for photographic designs, but vector formats provide the most precise cutting results.</p>

<h3>Can die-cut stickers be used outdoors?</h3>
<p>Yes, die-cut stickers are designed for outdoor durability and can withstand weather exposure, UV light, and temperature variations. The premium vinyl material and weather-resistant adhesive make these stickers suitable for vehicle applications, outdoor signage, and equipment labeling that requires long-term performance.</p>

<h3>How small can die-cut stickers be made?</h3>
<p>Die-cut stickers can be produced in very small sizes, though intricate designs with fine details may require larger dimensions for optimal cutting precision. Simple shapes and text can be cut quite small, while complex designs with multiple cut lines work best at larger sizes to maintain detail clarity.</p>

<h3>Do die-cut stickers leave residue when removed?</h3>
<p>Die-cut stickers are designed to remove cleanly from most surfaces when applied to appropriate materials. However, adhesive residue may occur on some surfaces depending on the material type, application duration, and removal method. Testing on a small area first is recommended for sensitive surfaces.</p>
