<h2>Die-Cut vs Kiss-Cut Dry Erase Sticker Options</h2>

<p>Understanding the difference between die-cut and kiss-cut options helps you choose the right cutting method for your dry erase sticker application. The main distinction lies in how the paper backing is handled during the cutting process.</p>

<h3>Die-Cut Dry Erase Stickers</h3>
<p>Die-cut means we cut through both the vinyl sticker material and the paper backing, creating individually cut stickers with no excess material around the design. Think of it like a "cookie cutter" approach - your dry erase stickers are precisely cut to the contour of your design.</p>

<p>This cutting method is ideal for custom shapes, logos, or when you want a clean, professional appearance without border material. Each dry erase sticker peels away as a separate piece, perfect for individual application or distribution.</p>

<p>Note: Back Slit (Crack and Peel) feature is available only on Standard and Permanent material options for easier individual sticker removal from sheets.</p>

<h3>Kiss-Cut Dry Erase Stickers</h3>
<p>Kiss-cut means we cut only through the vinyl layer (the sticker material) without cutting through the paper backing. This leaves a paper material border around your dry erase sticker, making it easier to peel and handle during application.</p>

<p>This option works well when you need easy peeling capability or when applying multiple stickers in sequence. The paper backing provides stability and makes it simple to position your dry erase stickers accurately on the target surface.</p>

<h2>Dry Erase Sticker Artwork Requirements & File Guidelines</h2>

<p>Proper file preparation ensures your dry erase stickers print with sharp details and accurate colors. Follow these guidelines to achieve professional results and avoid production delays.</p>

<h3>Preferred File Types</h3>
<p>For best quality dry erase sticker printing, submit vector-based files when possible. Our preferred formats include Adobe Illustrator EPS (Encapsulated Post Script) and PDF (Adobe PDF). Maximum file size accepted is 100 MB.</p>

<p>When submitting these file types, convert all fonts to outlines or curves to ensure they print correctly and prevent font substitution issues during production.</p>

<h3>Additional File Formats</h3>
<p>We also accept high-resolution raster files including JPG (JPEG), TIFF, and PNG formats. These work well for photographic elements or when vector files aren't available.</p>

<h3>File Setup Guidelines</h3>
<p>Set up your artwork at the exact proportional size you're ordering. Convert all text to outlines to prevent font issues. Allow for bleed only if necessary for your design. Never flatten or merge layers in PSD files, and clearly indicate cut lines for all dry erase sticker products.</p>

<h3>Vector vs Raster Artwork</h3>
<p>Vector art consists of mathematically defined lines and curves that can be enlarged to any size while maintaining crisp details. This format is ideal for text and drawn shapes on your dry erase stickers.</p>

<p>Raster images are made of colored pixels and work well for photographs. For optimal print quality, submit raster files at 100% scale (full size output) at 100 DPI, 50% scale at 200 DPI or higher, or 25% scale at 300 DPI or higher.</p>

<h2>Dry Erase Sticker Installation & Application Guide</h2>

<p>Proper installation ensures your dry erase stickers adhere well, function correctly, and can be removed cleanly when needed. Follow these steps for best results on various surface types.</p>

<h3>Preparing the Surface</h3>
<p>Clean the application surface thoroughly with a mild detergent solution to remove dust, oils, and residue. Allow the surface to dry completely before applying your dry erase stickers. Smooth, non-porous surfaces like glass, metal, and painted walls work best.</p>

<p>Ensure the surface temperature is between 50-90°F for optimal adhesive performance. Avoid applying dry erase stickers to freshly painted surfaces - allow paint to cure for at least 30 days.</p>

<h3>Applying the Dry Erase Sticker</h3>
<p>Peel the backing paper slowly while applying the sticker to avoid air bubbles. Start from one edge and use a squeegee or credit card to smooth the sticker as you apply it. Work out any air bubbles toward the edges during installation.</p>

<p>For larger dry erase stickers, consider using the hinge method: tape one edge in position, fold back the sticker, remove backing paper, then smooth into place while removing the tape.</p>

<h3>Removing Transfer Tape</h3>
<p>If your dry erase stickers include transfer tape, remove it slowly at a 45-degree angle after installation. Pull the tape back over itself rather than straight up to ensure the sticker remains in place.</p>

<p>Allow 24 hours for full adhesive cure before writing on your dry erase stickers. This ensures the surface coating is ready for marker use and cleaning without damage.</p>

<h2>Dry Erase Sticker FAQs</h2>

<h3>What material is used for dry erase stickers?</h3>
<p>Dry erase stickers are made from high-quality vinyl with a special laminated coating that creates a smooth, writable surface. This coating allows dry erase markers to write clearly and wipe clean easily without staining or ghosting over time.</p>

<h3>What surfaces work best for dry erase stickers?</h3>
<p>Dry erase stickers adhere best to smooth, clean, non-porous surfaces such as painted walls, glass, metal filing cabinets, desks, and whiteboards. Avoid textured or porous surfaces like fabric, unfinished wood, or rough concrete as these may affect adhesion and marker performance.</p>

<h3>Are dry erase stickers repositionable and removable?</h3>
<p>Yes, dry erase stickers can be carefully repositioned during initial application and removed cleanly from most surfaces without leaving adhesive residue. The removable adhesive is designed to provide good holding power while allowing clean removal when the sticker is no longer needed.</p>

<h3>What are the size limitations for custom dry erase stickers?</h3>
<p>We can produce dry erase stickers in a wide range of sizes from small individual labels to large wall-sized applications. Minimum size is typically 1 inch, while maximum dimensions depend on your specific requirements - contact our team for oversized dry erase sticker capabilities.</p>

<h3>How long do dry erase stickers last with regular use?</h3>
<p>With proper care and normal dry erase marker use, these stickers maintain their writing and erasing capabilities for months to years depending on usage frequency. The vinyl material is durable and the coating resists wear from regular cleaning with dry erasers or damp cloths.</p>

<h3>Can I use any type of dry erase marker on these stickers?</h3>
<p>Standard dry erase markers work best with our dry erase stickers. Avoid permanent markers, wet-erase markers, or other writing instruments as these may damage the coating or leave permanent marks that cannot be erased.</p>

<h3>What cutting options are available for dry erase stickers?</h3>
<p>We offer die-cut options for precise custom shapes that follow your design outline, and kiss-cut options that leave a paper border for easier peeling and handling. Choose the cutting method that best fits your application needs and installation preferences.</p>
