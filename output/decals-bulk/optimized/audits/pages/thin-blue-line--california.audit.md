<p>Position your State of California decal using the transfer tape, measuring and adjusting for proper alignment before removing the backing. <h3>Removing Transfer Tape</h3>
<p>Once your State of California decal is firmly applied, slowly remove the transfer tape at a low angle. <h2>State of California Decal FAQs</h2>

<h3>How long do State of California decals last outdoors?</h3>
<p>State of California decals are made from durable vinyl material designed for outdoor use and typically last 3-5 years when applied to clean, smooth surfaces like vehicle glass or metal. The matte vinyl finish provides additional protection against UV rays and weather exposure, helping maintain the thin blue line design colors and preventing fading or cracking under normal outdoor conditions.</p>

<h3>What surfaces work best for State of California decals?</h3>
<p>State of California decals adhere best to smooth, non-porous surfaces including glass, metal, plastic, and painted surfaces. Vehicle windows, equipment cases, laptops, and toolboxes provide excellent adhesion. Avoid applying to textured, porous, or heavily curved surfaces as these can reduce adhesion strength and affect the decal's appearance.</p>

<h3>Can State of California decals be removed without damage?</h3>
<p>Yes, State of California decals can typically be removed from most surfaces without permanent damage. Gently heating the decal with a hair dryer or heat gun softens the adhesive, making removal easier. Any adhesive residue can usually be cleaned with adhesive remover or rubbing alcohol. However, removal ease depends on the surface type and how long the decal has been applied.</p>

<h3>Are State of California decals available in different sizes?</h3>
<p>Currently, State of California thin blue line decals are available in a standard 5-inch size measured at the widest point of the state outline. This size provides excellent visibility while maintaining clear detail in the thin blue line design. The proportions are accurately scaled to match California's geographic shape.</p>

<h3>How should I clean State of California decals after application?</h3>
<p>Clean State of California decals using mild soap and water with a soft cloth or sponge. Avoid abrasive cleaners, scrubbing pads, or high-pressure washing directly on the decal, as these can damage the vinyl material or cause edges to lift. The matte finish is designed to resist fingerprints and maintain its appearance with regular gentle cleaning.</p>

<h3>Do State of California decals work on vehicle windows?</h3>
<p>Yes, State of California decals are excellent for vehicle window applications. The vinyl material adheres well to automotive glass and is designed for outdoor exposure to weather, road conditions, and car washing. Ensure the window surface is clean and dry before application for best results and maximum adhesion strength.</p>

<h3>What is the difference between die-cut and kiss-cut State of California decals?</h3>
<p>State of California decals are die-cut, meaning they're cut completely through both the vinyl and backing material, creating individual pieces that match the exact state outline. This differs from kiss-cut decals, which only cut through the vinyl layer while leaving the backing intact. Die-cut provides the cleanest appearance for state-shaped decals since only the California outline appears without excess background material.</p>
