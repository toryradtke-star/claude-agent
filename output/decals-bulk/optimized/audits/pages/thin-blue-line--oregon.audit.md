<h2>State of Oregon Installation Guide</h2>

<p>Proper installation ensures your State of Oregon decals maintain strong adhesion and professional appearance throughout their service life. Follow these installation steps for optimal results on vehicles, equipment, and other smooth surfaces.</p>

<h3>Preparing the Surface</h3>
<p>Clean the intended application surface thoroughly using warm soapy water, rubbing alcohol, or standard cleaning products. Remove all dirt, debris, wax, or other contaminants that could interfere with adhesive bonding. Allow the surface to dry completely before proceeding with State of Oregon decal installation.</p>

<p>Surface temperature should be between 50°F and 90°F for optimal adhesive performance. Avoid installation during extreme temperature conditions or high humidity, as these conditions can affect initial adhesive bonding strength.</p>

<h3>Applying the State of Oregon Decal</h3>
<p>Position the decal on the cleaned surface and use masking tape to hold placement before removing the backing paper. For precise alignment, measure and mark the intended position before final application. Remove the white backing paper slowly while applying pressure to prevent air bubble formation.</p>

<p>Use a plastic application tool, credit card, or squeegee to smooth the State of Oregon decal from the center outward. Apply firm, consistent pressure to ensure complete adhesive contact, paying special attention to detailed areas of the state outline.</p>

<h3>Final Installation Steps</h3>
<p>After complete application, press down any loose edges and eliminate air bubbles by working them toward the nearest edge. Allow the State of Oregon decal to set for 24 hours before exposure to harsh weather conditions for maximum adhesive strength development.</p>
