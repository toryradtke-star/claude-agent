Die Cut, Kiss Cut & Transfer Cut Calandered Vinyl Options

The primary difference between these three calandered vinyl material cut types relates to how the paper backing is handled during the cutting process. Each option serves different application methods and installation preferences.

Die Cut Calandered Vinyl Material

Die-cut calandered vinyl involves cutting through both the vinyl material and paper backing, similar to a cookie cutter approach. This method produces individually cut decals with no excess paper material around the design. Your calandered vinyl graphics are contour-cut to match your design exactly, providing clean individual pieces ready for application. Back slit (crack and peel) backing is available on standard and permanent adhesive options.

Kiss Cut Calandered Vinyl Material

Kiss-cut calandered vinyl material involves cutting only through the vinyl layer while leaving the paper backing intact. This creates a paper border around your decal, making removal and positioning easier during installation. The extra backing material provides a convenient grip area when applying your calandered vinyl graphics to target surfaces.

Transfer Cut Calandered Vinyl Material

Transfer-cut calandered vinyl material involves cutting the vinyl to specific shapes or text, then removing unwanted vinyl sections while applying transfer tape over the remaining design elements. This method works well for complex multi-piece designs and vinyl lettering applications. Transfer cutting requires vector files in EPS or Adobe Illustrator format for optimal results.

Transfer cut pricing includes 8 standard picks with a maximum of 40 picks per square foot. Additional picks are charged $0.02 to $0.04 per pick depending on complexity. Intricate designs receive custom quotes based on difficulty and time requirements. Review our transfer cut guidelines for detailed specifications.

Calandered Vinyl Material Artwork Requirements

Proper file preparation ensures optimal print quality and accurate color reproduction for your calandered vinyl material projects. Our production process accommodates various file formats while maintaining specific requirements for best results.

Preferred File Types

For calandered vinyl material printing, we recommend Adobe Illustrator EPS (Encapsulated Post Script) and PDF (Adobe PDF) formats. These vector-based files provide the highest quality output and maintain crisp edges at any size. When submitting these files, convert all fonts to outlines or curves to prevent font substitution issues during production.

Maximum accepted file size is 100 MB for all calandered vinyl material artwork submissions.

Additional File Formats

We also accept high-resolution JPG, TIFF, and PNG files for calandered vinyl material projects. These raster formats work well for photographic content and complex imagery, though vector files remain preferred for text and simple graphics.

File Setup Tips

Set up your calandered vinyl material artwork proportionate to your exact order size. Convert all text elements to outlines and include bleed only when necessary. Never flatten or merge layers in PSD files, and clearly indicate cut lines for all decal products. These practices ensure smooth production and accurate final results.

Transfer Cut Guidelines

Transfer cut calandered vinyl material requires special consideration for optimal results. Use simple, open fonts with all text at least 1/2" tall. Maintain minimum 1/8" spacing between characters and design elements. Main strokes, line weights, and art elements should measure at least 1/8" wide, with minimum pick size of 1/8". We recommend requesting a proof for all transfer cut calandered vinyl projects.

Calandered Vinyl Material Installation Instructions

Proper installation ensures maximum adhesion and longevity for your calandered vinyl material decals and graphics. Following these steps will help achieve professional results and prevent common application issues.

Preparing the Surface

Clean the target surface thoroughly with isopropyl alcohol or a similar degreasing agent before applying calandered vinyl material. Remove all dirt, wax, and contaminants that could interfere with adhesion. Allow the surface to dry completely before proceeding with installation.

Ensure the surface temperature is between 50-90°F for optimal calandered vinyl material adhesion. Cold surfaces can prevent proper bonding, while excessive heat may cause premature adhesive activation.

Applying the Calandered Vinyl Material

Position your calandered vinyl material carefully before removing the backing. For larger graphics, use the hinge method by taping one edge and gradually removing backing while smoothing the vinyl. Work from center outward to eliminate air bubbles and ensure even adhesion.

Use a squeegee or application tool to firmly press the calandered vinyl material against the surface. Pay special attention to edges and curved areas where lifting is most likely to occur.

Removing Transfer Tape

For transfer-cut calandered vinyl material, remove the transfer tape slowly at a 180-degree angle. If vinyl begins lifting with the tape, press the problem area down and continue removal more slowly. Allow the installation to cure for at least 24 hours before exposure to harsh conditions.

What Are Calandered Vinyl Materials?

Calandered vinyl materials are durable plastic films manufactured through a calendering process where heated vinyl compound is pressed through a series of heated rollers to achieve consistent thickness and smooth surface finish. This 3 mil material combines flexibility with strength, making it ideal for outdoor signage, vehicle graphics, and custom decal applications. Calandered vinyl features excellent printability and can conform to moderately curved surfaces while maintaining strong adhesive bonds. The material typically includes lamination protection to enhance durability and UV resistance for extended outdoor use.

Calandered Vinyl Material FAQs

What is the lifespan of calandered vinyl material?

Calandered vinyl material typically lasts 3-5 years outdoors when properly applied to clean surfaces and protected with lamination. Indoor applications can last significantly longer, often 7-10 years or more depending on environmental conditions. The laminated surface provides UV protection and scratch resistance to extend the material's useful life.

What surfaces work best with calandered vinyl material?

Calandered vinyl material adheres best to smooth, non-porous surfaces such as glass, metal, painted surfaces, and high-quality plastics. Clean automotive paint, storefront windows, and smooth metal signs provide excellent adhesion. Avoid textured, porous, or low-energy surfaces like untreated polyethylene where adhesion may be compromised.

Does calandered vinyl material have a glossy or matte finish?

Our calandered vinyl material features a glossy finish that enhances color vibrancy and provides easy cleaning. The smooth, glossy surface reflects light evenly and resists dirt accumulation better than matte alternatives. This finish works well for most signage and vehicle graphic applications where visual impact is important.

How small or large can calandered vinyl material decals be made?

Calandered vinyl material decals can be produced from as small as 1 inch up to 54 inches wide by virtually any length. Small detailed graphics maintain sharp edges and crisp text, while large format applications benefit from the material's conformability and strong adhesive properties. Custom sizing accommodates most project requirements.

Will calandered vinyl material damage vehicle paint?

High-quality calandered vinyl material with appropriate adhesive will not damage properly cured automotive paint when removed correctly. The removable adhesive allows clean removal within the material's intended lifespan. However, permanent adhesive options may leave residue or cause paint issues, so choose removable grades for vehicle applications.

How do you remove air bubbles from calandered vinyl material?

Remove air bubbles from calandered vinyl material by pricking small bubbles with a fine needle and pressing out the trapped air. For larger bubbles, lift the affected area slightly and reapply with proper squeegee technique. Working from center outward during initial application prevents most bubble formation.

What file format works best for calandered vinyl material printing?

Vector files in EPS or PDF format work best for calandered vinyl material printing because they maintain crisp edges and accurate colors at any size. These formats prevent pixelation and ensure professional results. High-resolution raster files (300 DPI minimum) are acceptable for photographic content but vector files remain preferred for text and logos.
