Cut Options for Custom Snowmobile Decals

The main differences between snowmobile decal cut options relate to how the vinyl material and paper backing are cut, affecting how you apply and handle your finished decals.

Die-Cut Snowmobile Decals

Die-cut snowmobile decals are cut completely through both the vinyl material and paper backing, creating individually cut decals with no excess material around your design. Think of this like using a cookie cutter - your snowmobile decals are precisely cut to the contour of your design for clean, professional application.

Back Slit (Crack and Peel) backing is available on Standard and Permanent vinyl materials for easier application of larger snowmobile decals. This option works particularly well for crack and peel applications.

Kiss-Cut Snowmobile Decals

Kiss-cut snowmobile decals are cut only through the vinyl layer, leaving the paper backing intact and creating a border of paper material around your decal. This extra material makes it easier to handle and position your snowmobile decals during application, especially for smaller registration numbers or detailed graphics.

Transfer-Cut Snowmobile Decals

Transfer-cut snowmobile decals involve cutting the vinyl to your specified design, then removing unwanted vinyl material and applying transfer tape over the remaining pieces. This method is ideal for snowmobile lettering, detailed logos, and multi-piece designs that need to be applied as a single unit.

Transfer-cut pricing includes 8 standard picks with a maximum of 40 picks per square foot. Additional picks range from $0.02 to $0.04 per pick depending on complexity. Vector files (EPS or Adobe Illustrator) are required for transfer-cut snowmobile decals.

Artwork Requirements for Snowmobile Decal Printing

Proper artwork preparation ensures your snowmobile decals print with sharp detail and accurate colors. Follow these guidelines for the best results with your snowmobile decal artwork.

Preferred File Types

Adobe Illustrator EPS (Encapsulated PostScript) and PDF (Adobe PDF) files produce the highest quality snowmobile decals. When submitting these files, convert all fonts to outlines or curves to ensure accurate printing and prevent font substitution issues.

Maximum file size accepted is 100 MB for snowmobile decal artwork uploads.

Additional File Formats

High-resolution JPG, TIFF, and PNG files are also accepted for snowmobile decal printing. These raster image formats work well for photographic elements but may have size limitations compared to vector artwork.

File Setup Tips

Set up your snowmobile decal artwork proportionate to the exact size you're ordering. Convert all text to outlines to prevent font issues during production. Allow for bleed only if necessary, and never flatten or merge layers in PSD files. Clearly indicate cut lines for all snowmobile decal products.

Transfer-Cut Guidelines

Transfer-cut snowmobile decals require specific design considerations. Use simple, open fonts with all text at least 1/2" tall. Maintain 1/8" minimum spacing between characters and design elements. Main strokes and line weights should be at least 1/8" wide, with a minimum pick size of 1/8".

We recommend requesting a proof for transfer-cut snowmobile decals to ensure your design translates properly to the cutting process. Consider our custom holographic vinyl options for unique snowmobile customization effects.

How to Install Snowmobile Decals

Proper installation ensures your snowmobile decals adhere securely and look professional on your snowmobile's surface. Follow these step-by-step instructions for best results with your snowmobile decal application.

Preparing the Surface

Clean the snowmobile surface thoroughly with soap and water, then wipe with isopropyl alcohol to remove any wax, grease, or residue. The surface must be completely dry before applying snowmobile decals. Ensure the temperature is above 50°F for optimal adhesive performance.

Applying the Snowmobile Decal

Remove the backing paper from your snowmobile decal and position it carefully on the cleaned surface. For larger decals, use the hinge method by taping one edge and gradually removing the backing while smoothing the decal with a squeegee or credit card. Work from the center outward to eliminate air bubbles.

Removing Transfer Tape

For transfer-cut snowmobile decals, slowly peel away the transfer tape at a 45-degree angle after the decal is fully applied. If any vinyl lifts with the tape, press it back down and continue removing the transfer tape more slowly.

Allow newly applied snowmobile decals to cure for 24-48 hours before exposing them to extreme weather conditions. This curing time helps ensure maximum adhesive bond strength for long-lasting performance on your snowmobile.

Snowmobile Decal FAQs

How long do snowmobile decals last outdoors?

Most snowmobile decals last 3-7 years outdoors when applied to clean surfaces and made from quality vinyl materials. Exposure to extreme cold, UV rays, and moisture may affect longevity, but premium vinyl materials resist fading and cracking in harsh winter conditions typical of snowmobiling environments.

What surfaces work best for snowmobile decals?

Snowmobile decals adhere best to smooth, clean surfaces like painted metal, fiberglass, and plastic body panels commonly found on snowmobiles. Clean the surface thoroughly and ensure it's dry before application for optimal adhesion and longevity.

Can snowmobile decals be removed without damaging paint?

Yes, snowmobile decals can typically be removed without damaging factory paint when removed properly using heat and adhesive remover. Gently warm the decal with a hair dryer and peel slowly to minimize paint risk, especially on older or repainted surfaces.

What file format works best for snowmobile decal artwork?

Vector files in EPS or Adobe Illustrator format work best for snowmobile decals because they can be scaled to any size without quality loss. High-resolution PNG, JPG, or TIFF files also work well for photographic elements in your snowmobile decal design.

Do you offer rush production for snowmobile decals?

Yes, rush production options are available with 1-day priority service for urgent snowmobile decal orders. Standard production takes 3-5 business days, while express options can complete your decals in 1-2 business days after artwork approval and payment.

Can snowmobile decals include registration numbers?

Absolutely, snowmobile decals are commonly used for registration number display and can be customized with specific fonts, colors, and sizes to meet your state's requirements. Many customers order matching sets for consistent registration compliance and professional appearance.

What's the difference between die-cut and kiss-cut snowmobile decals?

Die-cut snowmobile decals are completely cut through both vinyl and backing for individual pieces with no excess material, while kiss-cut decals only cut through the vinyl layer, leaving paper backing around the design for easier handling and positioning during application.
