Cut Options for Custom Canning Labels

Understanding the difference between die-cut and kiss-cut options helps you choose the best finishing method for your canning labels. The main difference lies in how we cut through the materials and what backing remains with your finished labels.

Die-Cut Canning Labels

Die-cut canning labels are precision-cut through both the vinyl label material and the paper backing, creating individually separated labels with no excess material around the edges. Think of this like a cookie cutter approach - each label is completely cut out to match your exact design contour.

This cutting method works exceptionally well for canning labels because it creates a clean, professional appearance on curved jar surfaces. Die-cut labels conform better to round mason jars and provide a seamless look that's perfect for farmers market products or gift presentations.

Please note: Back Slit (Crack and Peel) feature is available on Standard and Permanent material options for easier label removal.

Kiss-Cut Canning Labels

Kiss-cut canning labels are cut only through the top vinyl layer, leaving the paper backing intact around your label design. This creates a border of backing material that makes the labels easier to peel and apply to your jars.

The kiss-cut method is ideal for canning labels when you're applying many labels in sequence, such as during large canning sessions or when labeling inventory for farmers market sales. The extra backing material provides a convenient grip for faster label application.

Canning Label Artwork Requirements

Proper artwork preparation ensures your custom canning labels print with crisp text, vibrant colors, and professional quality that will make your jars look their best. Follow these guidelines to achieve optimal results for your canning label designs.

Preferred File Types

For the highest quality canning labels, submit vector-based files whenever possible. Our preferred formats include Adobe Illustrator EPS (Encapsulated PostScript) and PDF files. When using these formats, convert all fonts to outlines or curves to ensure your text prints exactly as designed without font substitution issues.

Vector files are ideal for canning labels because they maintain sharp edges and crisp text at any size, ensuring your product names, dates, and ingredient lists remain perfectly readable on finished labels.

Additional File Formats

We also accept high-resolution raster files including JPG, TIFF, and PNG formats. These work well for canning labels that incorporate photographs of your products or detailed illustrations. Ensure raster files meet our resolution requirements for best printing results.

File Setup Guidelines

Set up your canning label artwork at the exact proportional size you're ordering. Convert all text elements to outlines to prevent font issues. Include bleed areas only when necessary for full-coverage background colors or images that extend to the label edges.

For canning labels with specific cutting requirements, clearly indicate cut lines in your artwork. This ensures precise cutting that matches your intended design, especially important for uniquely shaped labels or those with decorative borders.

Resolution Requirements

Submit raster images at 100% scale with 100 DPI minimum resolution, or proportionally higher resolution for smaller scale submissions. High-resolution artwork ensures your canning labels print with sharp detail and vibrant colors that will make your preserved goods look professionally packaged.

Applying Custom Canning Labels

Proper application technique ensures your custom canning labels adhere smoothly and maintain their professional appearance throughout storage and use. Follow these steps for best results when labeling your jars and containers.

Preparing the Jar Surface

Clean your jars thoroughly with soap and water, then dry completely before applying canning labels. Remove any existing adhesive residue or oil fingerprints that could prevent proper adhesion. For best results, allow jars to reach room temperature before label application.

Glass mason jars provide an ideal surface for canning labels, but ensure the surface is completely smooth and free from condensation. Even small amounts of moisture can prevent proper adhesion and cause label edges to lift over time.

Applying Your Canning Labels

Peel the label from its backing, starting from one corner. Position the label carefully on your jar, as repositioning may reduce adhesive strength. Apply firm, even pressure across the entire label surface, working from the center outward to eliminate air bubbles.

For curved mason jar surfaces, apply labels slowly and smooth as you go to ensure the vinyl conforms properly to the jar contour. Take extra care around jar shoulders and curved areas where the label needs to flex.

Finishing and Storage Tips

After applying canning labels, avoid handling the labeled jars for several hours to allow the adhesive to achieve full bond strength. Store labeled jars in cool, dry conditions when possible to maximize label lifespan.

Our waterproof canning labels resist moisture and temperature changes, making them suitable for refrigerated storage and pantry conditions. However, extreme temperature fluctuations may affect adhesive performance over extended periods.

Custom Canning Label FAQs

What material are canning labels made from?

Our canning labels are made from premium vinyl material with a strong adhesive backing designed for kitchen and food storage environments. The vinyl construction provides excellent resistance to moisture, oils, and temperature changes commonly found in pantries and refrigerated storage, ensuring your labels maintain their appearance and adhesion throughout the life of your preserved goods.

How long do canning labels last on mason jars?

When properly applied to clean, dry surfaces, our canning labels typically last 2-3 years in normal pantry storage conditions. Labels stored in refrigerated environments may last even longer due to reduced temperature fluctuations. The actual lifespan depends on storage conditions, handling frequency, and exposure to moisture or temperature extremes.

Can I use canning labels for freezer storage?

Yes, our vinyl canning labels perform well in freezer conditions and will maintain adhesion at temperatures down to 0°F (-18°C). However, we recommend applying labels at room temperature before placing containers in the freezer, as cold application may reduce initial adhesive bond strength.

What's the difference between die-cut and kiss-cut canning labels?

Die-cut canning labels are completely cut through both the vinyl and backing material, creating individual labels with no excess material around the edges. Kiss-cut labels are cut only through the vinyl layer, leaving a paper backing border that makes them easier to peel and apply during high-volume labeling sessions.

Do you offer waterproof canning labels?

All our canning labels are naturally waterproof due to their vinyl construction and marine-grade adhesive. They resist moisture from condensation, spills, and humid storage conditions, making them ideal for both pantry storage and refrigerated environments where condensation is common.

What artwork format works best for canning labels?

Vector files in EPS or PDF format provide the highest quality results for canning labels, especially for designs with text and simple graphics. High-resolution JPG, PNG, or TIFF files work well for photographic elements. Ensure all text is converted to outlines in vector files to prevent font substitution issues during printing.

Can I get custom shapes for my canning labels?

Yes, we can die-cut canning labels to custom shapes that match your design requirements. Custom shapes work particularly well for specialty products or when you want your labels to follow the contour of decorative elements in your design. Contact our design team to discuss your specific shape requirements.

How should I store unused canning labels?

Store unused canning labels in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and heat sources. Keep them in their original packaging or a sealed container to prevent dust accumulation and maintain adhesive quality. Properly stored labels typically maintain their quality for 1-2 years.
