Die Cut vs Kiss Cut Product Label Options

Understanding the difference between die cut and kiss cut product labels helps you choose the right option for your application. Both cutting methods use the same high-quality vinyl material and printing process, but they differ in how the labels are finished and how you apply them.

Die Cut Product Labels

Die cut means we cut completely through both the vinyl label material and the paper backing. This creates individually cut product labels with no excess material around the design - similar to using a cookie cutter. Each label matches the exact contour of your design, providing a clean, professional appearance when applied.

Die cut product labels work best when you want the label to follow your design shape exactly. Back slit (crack and peel) backing is available on Standard and Permanent adhesive options for easier application of individual labels.

Kiss Cut Product Labels

Kiss cut means we only cut through the vinyl layer while leaving the paper backing intact. This creates a border of backing material around each product label, making them easier to peel and apply. The backing material provides a convenient grip area for handling individual labels.

Kiss cut product labels are ideal when you need easy removal from the backing sheet or when applying multiple labels quickly. The backing border also helps prevent damage to label edges during handling and storage.

Product Label Artwork Requirements

Proper artwork preparation ensures your product labels print with exceptional quality and accurate colors. Our printing process works best with vector-based artwork, but we also accept high-resolution raster images when prepared correctly.

Preferred File Types

For optimal product label printing results, submit artwork in Adobe Illustrator EPS (Encapsulated PostScript) or PDF format. These vector formats maintain crisp edges and solid colors at any size. When submitting EPS or PDF files, convert all fonts to outlines or curves to prevent font substitution issues.

Additional File Formats

We also accept JPG, TIFF, and PNG files for product label artwork. These raster formats must be high resolution to avoid pixelation in the final printed labels. Submit raster files at 100% scale (full output size) at 100 DPI minimum, or proportionally higher resolution for smaller scale files.

File Setup Tips

Set up your artwork proportionate to the exact size product label you're ordering. Convert all text to outlines to ensure fonts print correctly. Only include bleed if necessary for your design. Never flatten or merge layers in PSD files - keep layers separate for easier editing if needed.

Clearly indicate cut lines for all product label designs. Vector artwork scales perfectly and maintains quality at any size, making it ideal for text and geometric shapes. Raster images depend on resolution, so enlarging beyond the original file size will reduce quality.

Product Label Installation & Application

Proper installation ensures your product labels adhere securely and maintain their appearance over time. Following these application steps helps achieve professional results whether you're labeling bottles, packages, or retail products.

Preparing the Surface

Clean the application surface thoroughly with isopropyl alcohol or degreasing cleaner. Remove all dirt, oil, moisture, and residue that could interfere with adhesion. Allow the surface to dry completely before applying your product labels. Room temperature surfaces work best for initial adhesion.

Applying Product Labels

For die cut product labels, remove the label from the backing and position carefully - the strong adhesive bonds quickly upon contact. For kiss cut labels, use the backing border to grip and position the label before pressing down. Start from one edge and smooth across to avoid air bubbles.

Removing Transfer Tape

If your product labels include transfer tape, remove it slowly at a sharp angle after application. Pull the tape back on itself rather than straight up. This technique helps ensure the label stays adhered to your product surface rather than lifting with the transfer tape.

For best results, avoid applying product labels in direct sunlight or extreme temperatures. Allow 24 hours for full adhesive cure, especially in cold conditions. Once properly applied, your product labels will maintain their bond and appearance for years.

What Are Product Labels?

Product labels are adhesive vinyl decals designed specifically for identifying, branding, and providing information about commercial products. These labels are typically made from durable vinyl material with strong adhesive backing that bonds permanently to bottles, packages, containers, and retail items. Product labels serve essential functions including brand identification, ingredient lists, usage instructions, and regulatory compliance information. They are commonly applied during manufacturing or packaging processes and must withstand handling, storage, and consumer use while maintaining legibility and appearance.

Product Label FAQs

What material are product labels made from?

Product labels are printed on high-quality vinyl material that provides excellent durability and color retention. The vinyl construction resists moisture, UV exposure, and normal handling while maintaining adhesive strength over time.

Do product labels have glossy or matte finish options?

Yes, you can choose between glossy and matte finish options for your product labels. Glossy finish provides vibrant colors and high shine, while matte finish offers a subtle, professional appearance that reduces glare and fingerprints.

What is the minimum order quantity for product labels?

We accommodate both small and large quantity orders for product labels. Contact our sales team for specific minimum quantities as they may vary depending on label size and complexity of your design.

How long do product labels last when applied outdoors?

When applied to clean, smooth surfaces, product labels typically last 3-5 years outdoors with minimal fading or adhesive failure. Indoor applications can last significantly longer depending on environmental conditions and surface preparation.

Can I get a proof before my product labels are printed?

Yes, we provide digital proofs for all custom product label orders. You must approve the proof before production begins, ensuring your labels meet your exact specifications for colors, text, and layout.

What surfaces work best for product label application?

Product labels adhere best to clean, smooth surfaces including glass, plastic, metal, and coated paper. Textured or porous surfaces may require special adhesive options for optimal bonding performance.

What file formats do you accept for product label artwork?

We prefer EPS and PDF vector files for the highest quality product label printing. We also accept high-resolution JPG, TIFF, and PNG files when prepared at proper resolution and scale for your label size.
