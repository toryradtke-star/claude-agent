Cut Options for Circle Label Sheets

Circle label sheets are produced using precision cutting methods that create clean, circular edges while maintaining proper adhesive backing performance. Our cutting process ensures each round label peels cleanly from the sheet backing for easy application.

Kiss Cut Circle Label Sheets

Kiss cutting is the standard method for producing circle label sheets. This process cuts through the vinyl label material and adhesive layer while leaving the backing paper intact. Kiss cut circle labels remain on the sheet until you're ready to peel and apply them, making storage and handling more convenient.

This cutting method works best for circle label sheets because it allows multiple labels to stay organized on a single sheet while providing easy removal when needed. The backing paper provides stability during shipping and storage.

Die Cut Circle Labels

Individual die cut circle labels cut completely through all layers including the backing material. While this option is available for single labels, kiss cutting is typically preferred for label sheet applications since it keeps multiple labels organized on one sheet.

Die cutting may be recommended for circle labels that need to be distributed individually rather than kept on sheets for organized application.

Circle Label Sheet Artwork Requirements

Creating high-quality circle label sheets requires properly formatted artwork files that account for circular dimensions and printing specifications. Our artwork guidelines help ensure your labels print with crisp details and accurate colors.

Preferred File Types for Circle Label Sheets

Vector-based files produce the best results for circle label sheet printing. We prefer Adobe Illustrator EPS (Encapsulated PostScript) and PDF files with fonts converted to outlines. These formats maintain sharp edges and clean text even when scaled for different circular label sizes. Maximum file size accepted is 100 MB.

Additional Circle Label Sheet File Formats

High-resolution raster files are also accepted for circle label sheets, including JPEG, TIFF, and PNG formats. For optimal print quality, raster images should be submitted at 100% scale with 100 DPI resolution, or proportionally higher resolution for smaller scale files (200 DPI at 50% scale, 300 DPI at 25% scale).

Circle Label Sheet File Setup Tips

Design your artwork proportionate to the exact circular dimensions you're ordering. Convert all text to outlines to prevent font substitution issues. Clearly indicate cut lines for proper circular cutting. Avoid flattening layers in PSD files, and include bleed only when specifically required for your label design.

Vector vs Raster Art for Circle Labels

Vector artwork consists of mathematical lines and curves that can be scaled to any size while maintaining crisp edges - ideal for logos and text on circle labels. Raster images are made of pixels and work best for photographs, but may show pixelation if enlarged beyond their original resolution. Choose the format that best matches your circle label sheet design needs.

Circle Label Sheet Installation Instructions

Applying circle labels from label sheets requires proper surface preparation and technique to ensure strong adhesion and professional appearance. Follow these installation steps for best results with your custom circle label sheets.

Preparing Surfaces for Circle Labels

Clean the application surface thoroughly with isopropyl alcohol or a mild detergent solution to remove dirt, oil, and residue. Allow the surface to dry completely before applying circle labels. Smooth surfaces like glass, metal, and plastic provide the strongest adhesion for vinyl circle labels.

Check that the surface temperature is between 50-90°F for optimal adhesive performance. Cold surfaces may prevent proper initial adhesion, while extremely hot surfaces can cause premature adhesive activation.

Applying Circle Labels from Sheets

Peel each circle label carefully from the backing sheet, starting at one edge to avoid tearing or deformation. Position the label on your target surface and press down from the center outward to eliminate air bubbles and ensure even contact with the surface.

Apply firm, consistent pressure across the entire circle label surface using a squeegee or smooth tool. This ensures maximum contact between the adhesive and the surface for long-lasting adhesion.

Circle Label Installation Tips

Work with circle label sheets at room temperature for easiest handling and application. If labels seem difficult to remove from the backing, gently warm the sheet to activate the release liner. Store unused circle label sheets flat in a cool, dry location to maintain adhesive quality.

Circle Label Sheet FAQs

What materials are circle label sheets made from?

Circle label sheets are made from high-quality vinyl material with strong adhesive backing. The vinyl provides durability and weather resistance, while the adhesive ensures reliable bonding to glass, metal, plastic, and other smooth surfaces.

How many circle labels fit on each sheet?

The number of circle labels per sheet depends on the label diameter and sheet size you choose. Our configurator shows exactly how many round labels will fit on your selected sheet dimensions during the ordering process.

What surfaces work best for circle labels from sheets?

Circle labels adhere best to clean, smooth surfaces including glass, metal, plastic, painted surfaces, and laminated materials. Textured or porous surfaces may reduce adhesion strength and longevity.

Can I get different designs on the same circle label sheet?

Each circle label sheet typically features identical round labels with the same design. For multiple designs, you would need to order separate sheets for each different circle label design.

How do I remove circle labels from the backing sheet?

Peel circle labels carefully from one edge of the label, lifting slowly to avoid tearing. If labels are difficult to remove, gently warming the sheet can help activate the release liner for easier peeling.

What file format works best for circle label sheet artwork?

Vector files in EPS or PDF format with fonts converted to outlines produce the best results for circle label sheet printing. These formats maintain sharp edges and clear text at any size.

How long do circle labels from sheets last outdoors?

High-quality vinyl circle labels typically last 3-5 years outdoors when applied to clean surfaces and exposed to normal weather conditions. Indoor applications can last considerably longer.

Can circle label sheets be stored long-term before use?

Store circle label sheets flat in a cool, dry location away from direct sunlight. Properly stored label sheets maintain their adhesive quality and printability for extended periods before application.
