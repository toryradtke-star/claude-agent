Label Sheets Artwork Requirements & File Specifications

Proper artwork preparation ensures your label sheets reproduce accurately with sharp details and vibrant colors. Our printing process accommodates both vector and raster artwork, though each format has specific requirements for optimal results.

Preferred File Types

For best results, submit artwork as Adobe Illustrator EPS (Encapsulated Post Script) or PDF files. These vector formats maintain crisp edges at any size and ensure accurate color reproduction. When submitting vector files, convert all fonts to outlines or curves to prevent font substitution during processing. Maximum file size accepted is 100 MB.

Additional File Formats

We also accept high-resolution JPG, TIFF, and PNG files for label sheets artwork. These raster formats work well for photographic content or complex designs that don't require vector precision. Ensure raster files meet our resolution requirements based on your final label size.

File Setup Requirements

Create artwork files at the exact proportional size of your ordered labels. For multiple labels per sheet, include proper spacing and alignment in your artwork file. Convert all text to outlines to ensure font accuracy. Include bleed areas only if your design extends to the label edges. Never flatten or merge layers in PSD files, and clearly indicate cut lines for custom shapes.

Resolution Guidelines for Raster Files

Raster images require sufficient resolution to prevent pixelation in the final printed labels. Submit files at 100% scale with 100 DPI minimum, 50% scale at 200 DPI or higher, 25% scale at 300 DPI or higher, or 10% scale at 600-1200 DPI for optimal results. Higher resolution ensures better print quality, especially for detailed graphics or small text.

Vector vs. Raster Artwork

Vector artwork consists of mathematically defined lines and curves that scale to any size without quality loss. This format works ideally for logos, text, and simple graphics. Raster artwork comprises colored pixels and works best for photographs or complex imagery with gradients and detailed color transitions.

Design Recreation Services

If you cannot provide print-ready artwork, our design team offers vectorization and recreation services starting at $15 for simple logos. Complex designs may require custom quotes. This service requires advance payment and includes file storage for future orders. Contact our design team to discuss your specific artwork needs and pricing.

Label Sheets Application & Surface Guidelines

Proper application technique ensures your custom labels adhere securely and maintain their appearance over time. Each label on your sheet can be removed individually and applied to clean, dry surfaces for best results.

Preparing the Surface

Clean the target surface thoroughly with isopropyl alcohol or mild detergent to remove dirt, oils, and residue. Allow the surface to dry completely before label application. Smooth, non-porous surfaces like glass, metal, and plastic provide the strongest adhesion, while textured or porous surfaces may reduce adhesive performance.

Ensure the surface temperature is between 50-90°F for optimal adhesive activation. Cold surfaces may prevent proper bonding, while extremely hot surfaces can cause adhesive failure or label distortion.

Applying Label Sheets Labels

Remove individual labels from the carrier sheet by lifting one corner and peeling slowly to avoid tearing. Position the label carefully on your target surface, as repositioning may reduce adhesive strength. Start application from one edge and smooth toward the opposite edge, working out air bubbles as you go.

Use firm, even pressure across the entire label surface to ensure complete adhesive contact. A smooth tool like a credit card or squeegee can help eliminate air bubbles and ensure uniform adhesion, especially for larger labels.

Best Application Surfaces

These label sheets work excellently on glass windows and doors, metal surfaces including equipment and vehicles, smooth plastic containers and products, and clean painted surfaces. The vinyl construction provides flexibility for slight surface curves while maintaining strong adhesion.

Avoid application to heavily textured surfaces, fabrics, or materials that flex significantly, as these conditions may cause label edges to lift over time. For outdoor applications, ensure labels are fully adhered before exposure to weather conditions.

Storage and Handling

Store unused label sheets flat in a cool, dry location away from direct sunlight. The carrier sheet protects labels during storage and maintains their readiness for application. Handle sheets carefully to avoid creasing or damaging individual labels before use.

Label Sheets FAQs

What material are label sheets made from?

Label sheets are manufactured using high-quality vinyl material with permanent adhesive backing. This vinyl construction provides durability, weather resistance, and strong adhesion to various surfaces including glass, metal, and plastic. The material maintains its color and integrity through normal handling and application processes.

How many labels can fit on one sheet?

The number of labels per sheet depends on individual label size and overall sheet dimensions. Common configurations range from 4 large labels to 20 or more smaller labels per sheet. During the ordering process, you can specify your preferred label size and quantity to optimize the sheet layout for your specific needs.

What surfaces work best for these labels?

Label sheets work excellently on smooth, clean surfaces such as glass, metal, plastic, and painted surfaces. The vinyl construction and permanent adhesive provide strongest bonding to non-porous surfaces. Textured or porous surfaces may reduce adhesive performance and are not recommended for long-term applications.

Can I create different label designs on the same sheet?

Yes, label sheets can accommodate multiple different designs on a single sheet layout. This approach works well for businesses needing various label types or sizes while maintaining cost efficiency. Specify your requirements during the design process to create mixed-design sheets tailored to your specific applications.

How should I store unused label sheets?

Store label sheets flat in a cool, dry location away from direct sunlight and extreme temperatures. Keep them in their original packaging or between protective sheets to prevent damage to individual labels. Proper storage maintains adhesive quality and prevents premature aging of the vinyl material.

What file formats work best for label sheet artwork?

Vector files such as Adobe Illustrator EPS or PDF formats provide the best results for label sheet printing. These formats maintain crisp edges and accurate colors at any size. High-resolution raster files (JPG, TIFF, PNG) also work well, especially for photographic content, but require proper resolution based on final label dimensions.

Can labels be removed and repositioned after application?

These labels use permanent adhesive designed for single-use application. While labels may be removable from some surfaces, repositioning typically reduces adhesive strength and may leave residue. For best results, position labels carefully during initial application rather than attempting to reposition afterward.
