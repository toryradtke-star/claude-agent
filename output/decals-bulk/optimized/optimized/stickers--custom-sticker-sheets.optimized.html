Cut Options for Sticker Sheets

Choose the cutting method that best suits your sticker sheet application and removal preferences. Each cutting option affects how individual stickers separate from the backing material and how they appear once applied to surfaces.

Die Cut Sticker Sheets

Die cutting creates individual stickers that separate completely from the backing sheet, cutting through both the vinyl face material and adhesive backing. This method produces standalone stickers that peel away cleanly, leaving only the sticker design when applied. Perfect for sticker sheets where individual elements will be distributed or applied separately.

Kiss Cut Sticker Sheets

Kiss cutting slices through the vinyl face material and adhesive while leaving the backing paper intact. This creates a weeding effect where unwanted vinyl can be removed while keeping your sticker designs on the backing sheet until ready for application. Ideal for detailed designs or when you want stickers to remain organized on the original sheet.

Transfer Cut Sticker Sheets

Transfer cutting combines precision cutting with application tape overlay, allowing complex sticker sheet designs to transfer as complete units. The transfer tape holds multiple sticker elements in precise alignment during application, then removes after installation. Best suited for sticker sheets with multiple coordinated elements that need exact positioning.

Sticker Sheet Artwork Requirements

Proper artwork preparation ensures your custom sticker sheets print and cut with precision. Vector-based designs provide the best results for clean edges and scalable graphics, while high-resolution raster images can accommodate photographic elements in your sticker sheet layout.

Preferred File Types

Submit your sticker sheet designs as Adobe Illustrator EPS or PDF files for optimal results. These vector formats maintain crisp edges during scaling and provide our cutting equipment with precise path information. Convert all text to outlines before submitting to prevent font substitution issues during production.

Additional File Formats

We also accept high-resolution JPG, TIFF, and PNG files for sticker sheets containing photographic elements. Ensure raster images meet minimum resolution requirements: 300 DPI at full size, 600 DPI at 50% scale, or 1200 DPI at 25% scale for optimal print quality.

File Setup Tips

Design your sticker sheet at the exact dimensions you're ordering, with individual sticker elements clearly defined by cut lines. Indicate bleed areas only where necessary, and maintain proper spacing between individual stickers to ensure clean cutting. For complex designs, include a separate layer showing cut paths for each sticker element.

Transfer Cut Guidelines

If selecting transfer cut options, ensure your sticker sheet design accounts for application tape overlay. Avoid fine details that might not transfer cleanly, and consider how individual elements will align during the transfer process. Include positioning guides if your design requires precise alignment of multiple sticker components.

Applying Sticker Sheets - Installation Guide

Proper installation techniques ensure your custom sticker sheets adhere smoothly and maintain durability over time. Clean surface preparation and correct application methods prevent air bubbles and ensure long-lasting adhesion for both indoor and outdoor applications.

Preparing the Surface

Clean the application surface thoroughly with isopropyl alcohol or mild detergent, removing all dirt, oil, and residue. Allow the surface to dry completely before applying stickers from your sheet. Smooth, non-porous surfaces like glass, metal, and painted surfaces provide optimal adhesion for vinyl stickers.

Applying Individual Stickers

Peel each sticker from the sheet backing, starting from one corner and removing the backing paper gradually. Position the sticker carefully, as repositioning may reduce adhesive strength. Use a squeegee or credit card to smooth the sticker from center outward, eliminating air bubbles during application.

Removing Transfer Tape

For transfer-cut sticker sheets, apply the entire design with transfer tape attached, then remove the tape slowly at a 45-degree angle. If sticker elements lift with the tape, press down and try removing the tape more slowly. The sticker should remain adhered to the application surface while the transfer tape pulls away cleanly.

What Are Sticker Sheets?

Sticker sheets are collections of multiple individual stickers arranged on a single backing material, typically made from durable vinyl with pressure-sensitive adhesive. These sheets allow multiple designs to be produced together efficiently while providing organized storage and distribution of various sticker elements. Sticker sheets can be die-cut, kiss-cut, or transfer-cut depending on the intended application method, with each cutting technique affecting how individual stickers separate from the backing material.

Custom Sticker Sheet FAQs

What surfaces work best for sticker sheets?

Custom sticker sheets adhere best to smooth, clean surfaces like glass, metal, painted walls, and plastic. Clean the surface with alcohol before application to ensure optimal adhesion. Textured or porous surfaces may reduce adhesive strength and longevity.

How many different designs can fit on one sticker sheet?

The number of designs depends on your sheet size and individual sticker dimensions. Most sticker sheets accommodate 5-50 individual designs, with larger sheets allowing more variety. Our design team can help optimize your layout for maximum design count.

What file format should I submit for sticker sheet artwork?

Adobe Illustrator EPS files provide the best results for sticker sheets, ensuring precise cutting and optimal print quality. PDF files are also acceptable for vector-based designs. Convert all text to outlines and include clear cut lines for each sticker element.

How long do vinyl sticker sheets last outdoors?

Our vinyl sticker sheets typically last 3-5 years outdoors when properly applied to suitable surfaces. UV-resistant inks and durable vinyl construction protect against fading and weather damage. Indoor applications can last significantly longer with proper care.

Can I mix different cut types on one sticker sheet?

Each sticker sheet uses one cutting method (die cut, kiss cut, or transfer cut) consistently across all elements. For mixed cutting requirements, consider ordering separate sheets with different cut specifications to achieve your desired results.

What's the difference between kiss cut and die cut sticker sheets?

Die cut sticker sheets cut completely through backing material, creating individual removable stickers. Kiss cut sheets only cut through the face vinyl, leaving backing paper intact for organized storage. Choose based on how you plan to distribute and apply your stickers.

Do you offer design services for sticker sheet layouts?

Yes, our design team can create or optimize your sticker sheet layout for as little as $15. We can recreate logos, adjust spacing, and organize multiple designs for optimal cutting and visual appeal. Contact our sales team for design service details.
