Fast Sticker Cut Options & Backing Types

Understanding cut options helps you choose the right fast sticker format for your application needs. We offer die-cut and kiss-cut options, each providing different backing characteristics and application methods for various use cases.

The primary difference between cutting methods relates to how the paper backing is treated during manufacturing. Your choice affects sticker handling, application, and final appearance after installation. For detailed explanations, visit our comprehensive crack and peel stickers guide.

Die-Cut Fast Stickers

Die-cut fast stickers are precision cut through both the vinyl material and paper backing, creating individually separated stickers with no excess material around the design perimeter. This "cookie cutter" approach produces clean-edged stickers that match your design contours exactly.

Each die-cut fast sticker peels away completely from surrounding material, making them ideal for precise placement and professional appearance. Back slit (crack and peel) functionality is available on Standard and Permanent material options for easier application of larger designs.

Kiss-Cut Fast Stickers

Kiss-cut fast stickers are cut only through the vinyl layer while leaving the paper backing intact. This process creates a paper border around each sticker design, providing easy grip areas for peeling and application.

The continuous backing material makes kiss-cut fast stickers easier to handle during application, especially for smaller designs or multiple sticker sheets. The paper border provides convenient peeling tabs while protecting sticker edges during shipping and storage.

Fast Sticker Artwork Requirements & File Setup

Proper artwork preparation ensures your fast stickers achieve optimal print quality and accurate cutting. We accept various file formats with specific technical requirements to maintain our rapid production schedules while delivering professional results.

Preferred File Types

For best fast sticker quality, submit vector-based artwork files including Adobe Illustrator EPS (Encapsulated Post Script) and PDF (Adobe PDF) formats. Maximum file size is 100 MB. Convert all fonts to outlines or curves before submission to prevent font substitution issues during production.

Vector artwork provides unlimited scalability without quality loss, making it ideal for fast stickers of any size. These mathematical-based graphics maintain crisp edges and smooth curves at any reproduction scale.

Additional File Formats

We also accept high-resolution raster files including JPG (JPEG), TIFF, and PNG formats for fast sticker production. These pixel-based images require specific resolution settings based on your final sticker size to ensure sharp printing results.

Submit raster artwork at 100% scale with 100 DPI minimum, 50% scale at 200+ DPI, 25% scale at 300+ DPI, or 10% scale at 400+ DPI resolution. Higher resolution files produce better fast sticker print quality but increase file transfer and processing times.

File Setup Tips

Create artwork proportionate to your exact fast sticker dimensions and convert all text elements to outlined paths. Indicate cut lines clearly for all sticker products, allowing necessary bleed areas only when required. Never flatten or merge layers in PSD files, as this limits our production flexibility.

Our prepress team reviews all fast sticker artwork for technical compliance and print optimization before production begins, ensuring your designs meet quality standards within chosen turnaround times.

Fast Sticker Installation & Application Guide

Proper installation technique ensures fast stickers adhere correctly and maintain their appearance throughout their service life. Surface preparation and application method significantly impact adhesion quality and longevity, regardless of your chosen material type.

Follow these installation steps for optimal fast sticker performance on various surfaces. Installation difficulty varies by sticker size, material type, and surface characteristics. Large format applications may benefit from professional installation services.

Preparing the Surface

Clean the installation surface thoroughly with isopropyl alcohol or appropriate cleaning solution to remove dirt, grease, and residue. Allow surfaces to dry completely before applying fast stickers. Surface temperature should be between 50-90°F for optimal adhesive activation.

Test adhesion on inconspicuous areas when applying fast stickers to sensitive or unknown surface materials. Some surfaces may require primer or specialized preparation for optimal bonding.

Applying Fast Stickers

Position fast stickers carefully before making contact with the surface, as repositioning becomes difficult once adhesive contact is made. For larger applications, use hinge method installation by securing one edge and gradually working across the design while removing air bubbles.

Apply firm, even pressure across the entire fast sticker surface using a squeegee or smooth application tool. Work from center outward to eliminate air bubbles and ensure complete adhesive contact.

Removing Transfer Tape

For transfer-applied fast stickers, remove the transfer tape slowly at a sharp angle while the adhesive is still warm from application pressure. If sticker elements lift with the transfer tape, reapply pressure and wait before attempting removal again.

Installation video guides are available for complex applications requiring specific techniques. Contact customer service for installation support or professional installer referrals in your area.
