State of New Jersey Decal Installation Guide

Proper installation ensures your State of New Jersey thin blue line decal creates a strong, lasting bond with the application surface. Following these proven steps will help you achieve professional results and maximum durability.

Preparing the Surface

Clean the intended surface thoroughly using warm water and soap, rubbing alcohol, or common household cleaners. Remove all dirt, debris, and moisture that could interfere with adhesion. Wipe the area completely dry with a clean cloth before applying your State of New Jersey decal.

Applying the State of New Jersey Decal

Position your decal on the surface and secure with masking tape to check alignment. Create a hinge with tape across the top edge, then slowly peel away the backing paper. Apply the decal from the center outward using firm pressure with a squeegee, credit card, or smooth applicator tool.

Removing Transfer Tape

Once your State of New Jersey decal is firmly applied, slowly remove the transfer tape at a 45-degree angle. Press down any loose edges and work out air bubbles for a smooth, professional finish. Your decal is now ready to display your support for New Jersey law enforcement.

How long do State of New Jersey decals last outdoors?

State of New Jersey decals are made from durable vinyl material that typically lasts 3-5 years outdoors when properly applied to clean surfaces. The fade-resistant inks and weather-resistant adhesive ensure your thin blue line decal maintains its appearance through various weather conditions.

What surfaces work best for State of New Jersey decals?

State of New Jersey decals adhere best to smooth, non-porous surfaces such as glass, metal, plastic, and painted surfaces. Popular applications include vehicle windows, laptops, phone cases, toolboxes, and smooth painted walls. Avoid applying to textured or porous surfaces for optimal adhesion.

Yes, State of New Jersey decals can be removed by gently heating with a hair dryer and slowly peeling from one corner. The vinyl material and adhesive are designed to remove cleanly from most surfaces without leaving residue when removed properly within the expected lifespan.
