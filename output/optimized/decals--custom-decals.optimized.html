Professional Finishing Options for Custom Decals

Choose the perfect finish and cutting method to match your specific application needs and aesthetic preferences.

Die-Cut Decals

Precision cutting through both vinyl and backing material creates individually separated decals with no excess material. Each piece follows your design's exact contours for a clean, professional appearance.

Kiss-Cut Decals

Cuts through vinyl layer only, leaving backing material intact around your design. This method provides easy weeding and application while maintaining design positioning on the backing sheet.

Transfer-Cut Decals

Advanced cutting and weeding process removes excess vinyl, then applies transfer tape for precise multi-piece installations. Perfect for detailed text and complex graphics requiring exact placement.

Surface Finish Options

Standard finish provides slight gloss that enhances color saturation and provides easy cleaning. Matte options available for specialty applications requiring reduced glare or premium appearance.

Back-slit options available on standard and permanent materials for easier installation on curved or textured surfaces.

How long do custom vinyl decals last outdoors?

Custom vinyl decals last 3-5 years outdoors when applied to clean, smooth surfaces like glass or metal. Our UV-resistant inks and weatherproof adhesive maintain color vibrancy and prevent cracking or peeling throughout their lifespan. Indoor applications typically last much longer due to reduced environmental exposure.

What surfaces work best for vinyl decals?

Vinyl decals adhere best to clean, smooth, non-porous surfaces including glass, metal, painted walls, and high-quality plastics. Textured or porous surfaces like unfinished wood or fabric may not provide adequate adhesion for long-term applications.

What file format should I use for custom decal artwork?

Vector files in Adobe Illustrator EPS or PDF format provide the best results for custom decals. These formats ensure crisp edges and accurate colors at any size. High-resolution JPEG, TIFF, or PNG files work well for photographic content but may show pixelation when enlarged.

What's the difference between die-cut and kiss-cut decals?

Die-cut decals are cut completely through both vinyl and backing material, creating individually separated pieces with no excess material around your design. Kiss-cut decals only cut through the vinyl layer, leaving the backing intact around your design for easier weeding and positioning.

Can I get a proof before my vinyl decals are printed?

Yes, digital proofs are provided for all custom decal orders before production begins. You'll receive an email with your proof for review and approval, allowing you to request any necessary changes before manufacturing starts.

How do I apply transfer decals properly?

Transfer decals include pre-applied transfer tape that holds cut elements in position. Clean the application surface, position the decal with transfer tape, smooth from center outward to eliminate air bubbles, then carefully remove the transfer tape leaving only the vinyl on your surface.
