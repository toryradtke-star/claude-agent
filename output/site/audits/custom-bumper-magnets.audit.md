## 14) FAQ

<div class="faq-section">
<h2>Frequently Asked Questions</h2>

<h3>Will bumper magnets stick to my vehicle's bumper?</h3>
<p>Bumper magnets require magnetic-receptive steel surfaces to adhere properly. Many modern vehicles feature aluminum, plastic, or composite bumpers that won't hold magnets. Test with a small magnet first, or consider our truck magnetic signs for steel door panels and body sections instead.</p>

<h3>How long do custom bumper magnets last outdoors?</h3>
<p>Our laminated bumper magnets typically last 3-5 years with proper care and maintenance. UV-resistant inks and protective lamination guard against fading and weather damage. Regular cleaning and removal during extreme weather extends lifespan significantly while maintaining professional appearance.</p>

<h3>Can I upload my own design for bumper magnets?</h3>
<p>Yes, we accept various file formats including vector files (.eps), high-resolution PDFs, and JPG images. Vector files with outlined fonts produce the best results. Our design team can review your artwork quality and provide optimization recommendations before production begins.</p>

<h3>Are bumper magnets safe for painted vehicle surfaces?</h3>
<p>Our magnetic material is specifically designed for automotive use and won't damage factory paint finishes when properly maintained. Remove magnets periodically for cleaning both the magnet and vehicle surface to prevent dirt accumulation. Avoid sliding magnets across painted surfaces during installation or removal.</p>

<h3>Do you offer bulk pricing for large bumper magnet orders?</h3>
<p>Yes, quantity discounts apply to larger orders, with significant savings available for business and organizational purchases. Contact our customer service team to discuss volume pricing, custom quotes, and extended payment terms for qualifying bulk orders and repeat business customers.</p>

<h3>Can bumper magnets go through automatic car washes?</h3>
<p>We recommend removing bumper magnets before entering automatic car washes to prevent potential damage or loss. High-pressure water, brushes, and chemical cleaners can affect magnetic adhesion and potentially damage the printed surface. Hand washing around magnets is the safest cleaning method.</p>
