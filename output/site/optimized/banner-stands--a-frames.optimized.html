What do people call an A-frame sign?

A-frame signs are commonly called sandwich board signs, sidewalk signs, or pavement signs. These terms all refer to the same portable, double-sided display system that businesses use for outdoor advertising. The "A-frame" name comes from the shape of the supporting structure that resembles the letter "A" when viewed from the side.

What are A-frame signs made of?

Our A-frame signs feature 4mm corrugated plastic (coroplast) panels with full-color digital printing, paired with durable sign holders. This lightweight yet sturdy material provides excellent weather resistance and maintains vibrant graphics throughout extended outdoor use. The holders are constructed from materials designed for stability and easy sign changes.

Are A-frame signs waterproof and weather-resistant?

Yes, these signs are completely waterproof and engineered for outdoor use in various weather conditions. The corrugated plastic material resists moisture, UV rays, and temperature fluctuations while maintaining structural integrity. The weather-resistant printing ensures colors remain vibrant even with continuous outdoor exposure and cleaning.

Are A-frame signs double-sided?

Yes, our A-frame signs feature double-sided printing for maximum visibility from all directions. This design ensures your message reaches both foot traffic and vehicle traffic approaching from either direction. Double-sided capability maximizes your advertising investment by providing twice the exposure area on a single sign.
