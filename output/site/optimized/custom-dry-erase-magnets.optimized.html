## 14) FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What materials are used in your custom dry erase magnets?

Our dry erase magnets feature 30 mil magnetic material with professional lamination over full-color printing. The magnetic base provides strong adhesion to ferrous surfaces, while the protective lamination creates an optimal writing surface that resists wear, moisture, and scratches. This construction ensures reliable performance through thousands of write-and-erase cycles while maintaining vibrant printed graphics.

Can I order custom sizes and shapes for dry erase magnets?

Yes, we offer custom sizing up to 24" x 24" to accommodate various applications from compact reminder notes to large planning boards. Shape options include standard rectangular with rounded or square corners, plus custom contour cutting that follows your design elements. Our team can help determine the optimal size and shape configuration for your intended use and magnetic surface requirements.

What file formats work best for dry erase magnet artwork?

Vector files like .eps with outlined fonts produce the highest quality results, though high-resolution PDF and JPG files also work well. Our prepress team reviews all artwork for print optimization and can provide recommendations for improvements. If you need design assistance, our professional graphic designers can create custom artwork or optimize existing files for best printing results.

How do I clean and maintain dry erase magnets?

Use standard dry erase markers and clean with dry erasers or soft cloths for regular maintenance. For thorough cleaning, use mild soap and water or dry erase board cleaners, avoiding abrasive materials that could damage the laminated surface. Proper maintenance ensures long-term performance and prevents marker ghosting. The laminated surface resists staining and maintains smooth writing characteristics through extended use.

What surfaces work best with dry erase magnets?

Dry erase magnets adhere to any magnetic-receptive surface including steel filing cabinets, refrigerators, whiteboards with steel backing, and metal doors. They won't stick to aluminum, plastic, or non-ferrous materials. Before ordering, verify your intended surface accepts magnets by testing with a standard refrigerator magnet. The 30 mil thickness provides strong holding power on appropriate surfaces.
