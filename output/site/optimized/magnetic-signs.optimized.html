## 14) FAQ
**What surfaces do magnetic signs work best on?**

Magnetic signs adhere to ferrous metal surfaces including steel vehicle panels, appliances, filing cabinets, and magnetic whiteboards. They won't stick to aluminum, plastic, or fiberglass surfaces. Before ordering, test your intended surface with a small magnet to verify compatibility. Steel vehicle doors and hoods provide excellent adhesion for mobile advertising applications.

**How long do magnetic signs typically last?**

With proper care and storage, our laminated magnetic signs maintain their appearance and adhesion for 3-5 years of regular use. Lifespan depends on exposure conditions, with indoor applications lasting longer than outdoor use. Regular cleaning and flat storage when not in use extend operational life. UV-resistant inks and protective lamination prevent fading and cracking.

**Are magnetic signs safe for vehicle paint?**

Quality magnetic signs won't damage vehicle paint when properly applied and maintained. Clean both the sign and vehicle surface before application, and remove signs regularly for cleaning underneath. Avoid dragging signs across painted surfaces, and store them flat when not in use. Rounded corners reduce wind lift and paint contact pressure.

**What's the maximum size available for magnetic signs?**

Our magnetic signs are available up to 24" x 24" maximum dimensions. This size limitation ensures proper magnetic adhesion and material stability. Larger advertising needs can be met with multiple coordinated signs or alternative signage solutions. Custom shapes within these dimensions are available through contour cutting options for unique design requirements.

**Can magnetic signs withstand weather exposure?**

Our laminated magnetic signs resist rain, snow, and UV exposure for reliable outdoor performance. The protective lamination shields graphics from moisture and prevents color fading. However, extreme weather conditions like hail or ice storms may damage any exterior signage. Remove signs during severe weather when possible to extend their lifespan.
